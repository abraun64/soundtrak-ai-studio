Operator Guide

Before the how-to — the thing that makes this different: you own it. This isn't a website you log into or an app you rent. It's a copy of the whole system that lives on your computer:
  • You own it — yours to run indefinitely, no seat licence, no lock-in.
  • It's all readable — how it works, your brand profile, and every asset it makes are plain files you can open, read, and change.
  • You run and control it — the AI does the work; you make the calls, and steer it in plain English.
  • Your data stays yours — on your machine; the only thing that leaves is what you type into a session.
It's a premium, licensed product — yours to run for your own business's marketing, not to resell or run for other people's clients. Transparent and yours to control, not open-source.

The AI studio that 10Xs your marketing.

Everything you do, stage by stage — from starting your first campaign to shipping each piece and keeping it running. You mostly answer questions and approve work in chat; this guide shows you what you'll see, what to get done, and exactly what you approve to move on. Written for someone who has never used the system before.

Four things worth knowing first:
  1. You're in charge. The studio drafts and checks the work, but nothing goes out without your yes. Push back whenever something feels off.
  2. It works in stages, on purpose. Your business was set up once during install (see the Deployment guide); from there you advance each campaign stage by approving it — that gate is the red box in every stage below.
  3. It's a conversation, not a wizard. You type plain‑English requests; it asks questions back and does the work. The screens shown here regenerate themselves — you read them, you don't maintain them.
  4. One chat per job. A fresh chat for each campaign, and another for each system retro. Your work lives in files, so a new chat always picks up where you left off.
Your journey at a glance

What you do

Answer the brief interview (short, ~15 min). Read the one‑page brief it drafts.

✅ You're done when

  • The goal and the one success measure are right.
  • The audience and offer match what you meant.

→ To continue, you approve

Say "approve the brief" (or send it back with a note). Approving fires the Creative Director and opens the concepts.

2Pick a creative concept (one big idea)Phase 2

The Creative Director comes back with three creative directions — one safe, one smart, one bold — with one recommended and the reasons why. You read them and choose. You can also steer ("sharpen the bold one") and it re‑works around your call.

Read all three and the recommendation's rationale. Decide which direction fits — or ask for a tweak first.

✅ You're done when

  • You've read all three and the "why" on the recommended one.
  • You've settled on the direction (after any tweak).

→ To continue, you approve

Reply "go with the Smart one" (or whichever). That locks the concept and the studio drafts the plan.

You don't approve from the screen — give your verdict in chat: "approve the landing page", or "send it back — tighten the subhead", or "archive/delete it". Repeat for each asset until all are approved.

Approve the launch plan, then work down it — publish where the studio is connected, follow the copy‑paste steps elsewhere, and do the final send yourself.

✅ You're done when

  • You've approved the launch plan.
  • You've completed the launch tasks.

→ To continue, you approve

Say "approve Phase 5 plan" before any step goes live. A one‑off campaign is done here; an ongoing one moves to Manage & Report.

6Manage & ReportPhase 6 · ongoing campaigns

For anything ongoing, the studio runs a simple weekly rhythm — one or two moves a week. You read the numbers it reports, make the occasional call, and it closes out at the end. (A one‑off campaign skips this stage.)

campaigns/acme-q3-leadgen/phase-6-cadence.html
WeekWhat you do
Wk 1Keep half an eye on the live pieces daily (~5 min). No decisions.
Wk 3First read (~30 min): the studio reports the numbers; small, reversible tweaks only.
Wk 4The big call (~60 min): shift budget from the weakest channel to the strongest. Write down why.
Wk 6Close the campaign: results report → retro → graduate the learnings → freeze.

What you do

Approve the ongoing‑management plan, then follow the weekly moves — read the studio's numbers, make the one budget call, and let it report + close out at the end.

✅ You're done when

  • You've approved the ongoing‑management plan.
  • You've completed this cycle's tasks.
  • You're following the weekly moves.

→ To continue, you approve

Say "approve Phase 6 plan", then approve each cycle as it comes. A closed campaign freezes — its learnings graduate up to sharpen the next one.

✦Improve the studioOngoing · optional

The studio can get better at your business over time. In a fresh chat named Retro, type /system-manager. Capture ideas as they occur, and run a retro every few weeks — the System Manager turns them into a prioritised to‑do list and tracks the history.

The studio also learns from the examples you give it. Save standout work as you spot it ("save this as a good example") and it joins your reference library — the collection of campaigns and pieces it draws on when creating your work. The library won't refresh on its own, but once a week the studio suggests fresh examples worth adding and reminds you if it hasn't grown in a couple of weeks, so it never quietly goes stale. You always decide what goes in.
