# 📣 Attention / fame

**Purpose**: earn broad, disproportionate attention — get the brand seen, talked about, and remembered by people who weren't looking for it.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: reach, earned-media / PR value, social conversation volume, brand recall, search-interest lift, "went viral"
- Audience state: unaware or indifferent; a low-interest category that needs an interrupt
- Brief signals: "we need to be famous," "punch above our budget," "get people talking," "launch with a bang," "a moment"
- Stage of brand: small-budget challenger vs. a big incumbent; a launch that needs oxygen; a category nobody finds interesting

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Dollar Shave Club — Our Blades Are Great (2012)** — one low-budget film made an unknown brand famous overnight
- **Burger King — Whopper Detour (2018)** — a stunt mechanic (a 1¢ Whopper ordered from inside a McDonald's) engineered for earned media
- **Cadbury — Worldwide Hide (2021)** — a participatory stunt built for press-scale reach
- **Heinz — Draw Ketchup (2021)** — a simple, shareable idea that dramatised brand ubiquity
- **Liquid Death — Murder Your Thirst (2019)** — attention as the entire strategy; the brand IS the joke
- **Barbie — The Movie (2023)** — total-saturation cultural moment

## What makes the mode work

- **One idea a stranger can retell in a sentence.** Fame travels by word of mouth; if it can't be retold, it doesn't move.
- **Built for earned, not paid.** The mechanic is designed to generate coverage and shares; paid amplifies a fire, it doesn't start one.
- **Distinctive, and attributed.** Attention that doesn't attach to the brand is wasted reach — the fame must be unmistakably *yours*.
- **A built-in social object.** Stunt, joke, spectacle, or provocation — a reason to talk is engineered in, not hoped for.

## Anti-patterns

- **Attention theatre**: a big spectacle nobody connects to the brand — reach with zero attribution.
- **Borrowed virality**: chasing a meme/trend with no brand fit; the audience shares the format and forgets the brand.
- **Fame-without-follow-through**: a massive spike with no funnel behind it to catch the demand.
- **Edgy-for-edgy**: provocation with no strategic point; buys attention, spends brand equity.

## When NOT to pick this mode

- The audience is already in-market and ready to buy — pick **Direct-response**
- You need to change a specific belief, not just get seen — pick **Aperture-setting**
- The KPI is qualified pipeline, not raw reach — pick **Educational pipeline** or **Trust / proof**
- You can't fund the follow-through to convert the attention — a smaller, fully-funded mode beats a spike you can't catch
