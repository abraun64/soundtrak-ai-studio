# 🤝 Community / advocacy

**Purpose**: turn existing customers into the marketing — a community that belongs, creates, and refers, so growth compounds through people rather than spend.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: referral / word-of-mouth share of new customers, user-generated content volume, community size + activity, retention + expansion, NPS
- Audience state: already has customers/fans with latent enthusiasm that isn't being channelled or amplified
- Brief signals: "our customers love us but don't talk about us," "grow through word of mouth," "build a community," "activate advocates"
- Stage of brand: has product-market fit + a base of genuine fans; wants growth that doesn't scale linearly with ad spend

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Glossier — customer-as-marketing (2014)** — the canonical case: customers became the content, the channel, and the R&D
- **LEGO — Rebuild the World (2019)** — community creativity as the engine; fans make the brand
- **Figma — Config + community (2017)** — a conference/community that turned users into evangelists
- **Duolingo — TikTok mascot (2021)** — a fandom the brand co-creates with, not just broadcasts to
- **Airbnb — host community** — supply-side advocacy as the growth loop

## What makes the mode work

- **Give people status, not just a discount.** The strongest advocacy is identity-driven — belonging and recognition, not a referral bribe.
- **Make participation easy and visible.** Low-friction ways to create/share, and public credit for those who do; visibility begets more.
- **Co-create, don't broadcast.** The brand joins the conversation and hands over some control — a community you fully control isn't one.
- **A compounding loop.** Each cohort of advocates recruits the next; the asset is the loop, not any single campaign.

## Anti-patterns

- **Astroturf**: manufactured "community" or paid advocacy the audience sees through — kills the trust it needs.
- **Extractive referral**: treating advocates as a free acquisition channel to be squeezed, with nothing given back.
- **Control freakery**: a "community" the brand micromanages — no real ownership, so no real energy.
- **Community-without-product-love**: trying to engineer advocacy before the product earns it; there's nothing to advocate.

## When NOT to pick this mode

- You don't yet have fans — earn love first (product, or **Trust / proof**); advocacy can't precede affection
- You need immediate conversions — pick **Direct-response**
- The goal is to reach strangers, not activate existing customers — pick **Attention / fame** or **Aperture-setting**
- You won't cede any control — community only compounds when the audience genuinely owns part of it
