# 🎯 Direct-response / conversion

**Purpose**: drive a measurable action with minimum friction. Compressed funnel optimised for the click, not the experience.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: conversions, signups, sales in a defined window, CAC efficiency, ROAS, app installs
- Audience state: ready to act now, friction is the enemy, ceremony works against you
- Brief signals: "flash sale," "launch window," "Q-end push," "performance marketing," "lower CAC by X%," "ad spend isn't pulling its weight"
- Stage of brand: known enough that the audience doesn't need to be sold on the brand — just on the offer

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Dollar Shave Club — "Our Blades Are F***ing Great" (2012)** — launch direct-response video that converted while building brand; rare combo
- **Casper — Sleep Channel + transit + podcast (2014+)** — performance marketing dressed as content; conversion-optimised at every step
- **Squarespace — Super Bowl run (Adam Driver, Zendaya years)** — high-craft, high-fame DR (the URL is the entire CTA)
- **Geico — long-running multi-platform (1994+)** — 15-second insurance quote machine, every asset converts
- **Liquid Death — DTC ecommerce funnel (alongside its participatory work)** — the participatory campaign drives the DR funnel
- **Mailchimp — Did You Mean Mailkimp (2017)** — also doubles as a DR exemplar — every channel had a clear path to trial

## What makes the mode work

- **Singular CTA.** One action, repeated everywhere. Multiple CTAs = none.
- **Friction audit on every touchpoint.** Every click, field, decision is justified or removed. Casper's Sleep Channel removed even the "should I buy a mattress?" decision.
- **Offer clarity over brand cleverness.** Buyers in DR mode care about the deal, the urgency, and the proof. Cleverness must serve clarity.
- **Measurable creative.** Each variant is tested; winners scale, losers die. The creative is in service of the data, not the other way around.
- **Performance-creative loop tight.** Concept-to-test-to-iterate cycle is days, not months.

## Anti-patterns

- **Brand pretending to do DR**: a brand film with a tacked-on CTA, measured by clicks. The film is too slow for DR audiences; the CTA is too thin for brand. Falls between two stools.
- **Multiple CTAs**: "learn more / get a demo / start free trial" — picks none.
- **High-craft, no clarity**: gorgeous campaign nobody can tell what to *do* about.
- **DR-as-spam**: blasting offers to cold lists; works for one quarter, kills the list for years.
- **Ignoring offer/copy in favour of "creative"**: in DR, offer beats art every time. Bad creative + great offer outperforms great creative + bad offer.

## When NOT to pick this mode

- Brand is unknown — DR doesn't convert cold; warm the audience first (Aperture or Educational)
- Audience is not in-market — DR depends on intent already being present
- Consideration is long — DR can't compress a 6-month buying cycle into a 30-day click
- KPI is awareness or attitudinal shift — wrong instrument
- Brand stretch tolerance is Tight and DR's brand-thinness would violate brand standards
