# 📚 Educational pipeline

**Purpose**: move buyers through a considered purchase decision. Sequenced touchpoints that reduce decision risk at each gate.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: pipeline created, qualified meetings, MQLs, SQLs, demo requests, sales cycle compression
- Audience state: actively evaluating (or about to), decision is non-trivial, buying committee exists, switching cost is real
- Brief signals: "demand gen," "thought leadership," "sales enablement," "ABM," "we need to compress sales cycles," "buyers are doing more research before talking to sales"
- Category: complex B2B SaaS, financial products, healthcare, considered consumer (mortgages, insurance, cars)

## Exemplars in the repository

- **HubSpot — Inbound Marketing methodology + INBOUND event (2006+)** — invented the category, then taught the audience how to practice it; pipeline as side effect of education
- **Drift — Conversational Marketing book + framework (2018+)** — gave the audience a new framework to think with, became the default vendor for that framework
- **Salesforce — Trailhead (2014+)** — gamified education turned customers into advocates and accelerated adoption
- **Mailchimp — Did You Mean Mailkimp? (2017)** — Cannes Grand Prix campaign that played as participatory but was a sophisticated B2B awareness-to-trial funnel
- **Gong — Reveal podcast + sales-call truth content (ongoing)** — data-backed POV content that pre-sells before any rep conversation
- **Stripe — Press page + docs as marketing (ongoing)** — craft + clarity replaced traditional sales motion for technical buyers

## What makes the mode work

- **Sequenced asset architecture.** Awareness → evaluation → decision, each stage with assets designed for that stage's job (POV piece → comparison guide → ROI calculator → demo). Skipping stages loses buyers.
- **Genuinely useful, not transparently promotional.** The asset has to earn its place in the buyer's research. Buyer-Beware-mode is on; transparent ad-as-education fails.
- **Frameworks beat features.** Giving the audience a new way to think (Inbound, Conversational, JTBD) is more durable than enumerating features.
- **Earned trust → consideration set inclusion.** The buyer's mental shortlist forms before they talk to sales. Educational pipeline is how you get on it.
- **Patient measurement.** ROI shows up over 6–18 month buying cycles, not in a 30-day window.

## Anti-patterns

- **Thin gating**: trading a thin whitepaper for an email address — buyers learned to use junk emails, signal is dead
- **Vendor-as-thought-leader theatre**: content that gestures at thought leadership but actually just sells. Buyers spot the seam.
- **Top-of-funnel only**: lots of awareness content, no evaluation-stage assets, sales reps end up doing the buyer's research for them
- **Lead-gen-by-volume**: optimising for raw MQL count instead of quality; sales team learns to ignore marketing leads
- **Conference-as-pipeline**: assuming an event will fill the funnel without the content engine around it

## When NOT to pick this mode

- Brand is unknown — buyer won't research a vendor they've never heard of. Need Aperture or Participatory first to build awareness.
- Purchase is impulse / low-consideration — pick Direct-response
- Buying committee is one person and the budget is small — the funnel overhead doesn't pay back
- KPI is in-window conversion — educational pipeline doesn't convert fast enough
