# 🎢 Participatory / movement

**Purpose**: pull the audience into a cultural moment they want to be inside of. Participation *is* the point — not a byproduct of consumption.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: share of voice, earned media, UGC volume, social mentions, "cultural moment" framing, organic share velocity
- Audience state: actively participates in culture / has identity tied to the category / shares for status
- Brief signals: "we want to be talked about," "viral moment," "cultural relevance," "earned-led campaign," "platform-native"
- Stage of brand: established enough to have permission to throw a party; or insurgent enough that audacity reads as authentic

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Spotify — Wrapped (2016+)** — gold-standard data-as-participation; users become the protagonists using their own behaviour
- **Burger King — Whopper Detour (2018)** — turned competitor's stores into the participation surface; audience had to act to get the joke
- **Old Spice — The Man Your Man Could Smell Like (2010)** — turned ad into back-and-forth with audience; participation through reply-videos
- **Heinz — Draw Ketchup (2021)** — invited the world to prove a brand insight; participation was the proof
- **Cadbury — Worldwide Hide (2021)** — turned Easter egg hunt into a global, location-based experience
- **Liquid Death — ongoing platform** — participation through irreverence; audience shares because the brand gives them social ammunition
- **Salesforce — Dreamforce** — B2B participatory at conference scale; identity-formation for the Salesforce ecosystem

## What makes the mode work

- **The audience does work.** Sharing, voting, posting, creating, going somewhere, looking up something. If passive, it's not participatory.
- **Status currency.** Sharing the campaign reflects something the audience wants to project about themselves (cleverness, taste, in-group membership, values).
- **A device that scales infinitely.** Wrapped's device (your data, your year) generates infinite variations. Bad participatory campaigns are unscalable stunts that only the lucky few get to be inside.
- **Platform-native craft.** The asset is shaped by the platform it lives on — vertical for TikTok, screenshot-worthy for Twitter/X, photo-frame-able for Instagram.
- **Earned >> paid.** Paid budget seeds the moment; participation carries it.

## Anti-patterns

- **Fake participation**: a hashtag with no actual participation surface. "#JoinTheMovement" with no movement.
- **Cringe attempt at culture**: brand chasing a meme three weeks late, or co-opting a subculture it has no permission in.
- **Unscalable stunt**: a clever activation 50 people experienced, dressed up as a participatory campaign by the case-study film.
- **Forced virality**: scripting "viral moments" — audiences sniff this out instantly.
- **No social currency**: asking the audience to share content that makes *them* look bad ("share this if you love our brand!").

## When NOT to pick this mode

- The brand has no permission to throw a party (too new, too quiet, too corporate, in a regulated space where playfulness reads as inappropriate)
- KPI is direct conversion within window — participation amplifies but doesn't convert reliably
- Audience is small/niche — participatory needs critical mass to create perceived movement
- Budget is too small to seed the moment — paid amplification matters in the first 72 hours
