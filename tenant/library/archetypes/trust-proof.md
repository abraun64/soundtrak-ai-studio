# 🧾 Trust / proof

**Purpose**: win skeptical, high-consideration buyers by making the brand's claims *believable* — evidence over assertion.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: conversion of considered / high-ticket purchases, sales-cycle acceleration, lead quality, win-rate against incumbents
- Audience state: doing diligence, risk-averse, has to justify the choice to someone else; has heard every claim already
- Brief signals: "long sales cycle," "buyers don't believe us," "we need proof," "credibility gap," "we look the same as everyone"
- Stage of brand: challenger selling into a trust-heavy or regulated category; a claim that sounds too good without backing

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Gong — data-as-marketing** — turns proprietary call data into the category's evidence base; the proof *is* the marketing
- **Netwealth — IQ research reports** — owns the category's data + conversation, so its claims land as fact, not opinion
- **Fletch — "Let's fix your confusing positioning" (website)** — results-led testimonials with quantified outcomes ([[fletch-pmm-website-2026]])
- Case-study- and demo-led B2B sites generally (named customers, real numbers, live proof)

## What makes the mode work

- **Proprietary evidence.** Data, research, or a demo competitors can't cite — proof they can't match beats proof they can copy.
- **Specificity.** Real numbers, named customers, quantified outcomes ("+25% conversion") — vague credibility reads as no credibility.
- **Show, don't tell.** Demos, teardowns, live results, receipts — let the audience verify rather than take your word.
- **Third-party voice.** Customers, analysts, and data say it; the brand steps back. Self-praise is discounted on arrival.

## Anti-patterns

- **Proof-by-adjective**: "trusted," "leading," "best-in-class" with nothing behind them — assertion dressed as evidence.
- **Vanity metrics**: cherry-picked numbers the audience can smell; erodes the trust it's meant to build.
- **Logo soup**: a wall of customer logos with no story — recognition without a reason.
- **Over-polished testimonials**: so scripted they read as fake; rough-but-real out-converts smooth-but-staged.

## When NOT to pick this mode

- The audience is unaware — get seen first with **Attention / fame** or **Aperture-setting**
- Low-consideration, impulse purchase — proof is friction; pick **Direct-response** or **Attention / fame**
- The goal is to deepen loyalty among people who already believe — pick **Identity reinforcement**
- You have no real proof yet — build the evidence before you build the campaign; manufactured proof is the fastest way to lose trust
