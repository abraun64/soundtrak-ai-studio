# 🧰 Utility / service

**Purpose**: earn attention and preference by being *useful* — a tool, calculator, dataset, or service the audience actually uses, not an ad they endure.

## When this mode is the right call

- KPI shape: repeat engagement, sign-ups / lead capture via the tool, backlinks + organic search, first-party data captured, assisted conversions
- Audience state: has a recurring job-to-be-done the brand can help with directly; ignores advertising but adopts things that help
- Brief signals: "give them something they'll keep using," "an always-on asset," "own a job in their workflow," "content that isn't promotional"
- Stage of brand: any — especially strong for compounding, always-on programs and for categories where the product itself is a kind of utility

## Exemplars in the repository

- **Google — Year in Search** — a recurring branded data-utility that people seek out and share
- **HubSpot — Inbound + free tools** (Website Grader et al.) — utility as the top of a self-serve funnel; every tool is also a sensor
- Branded calculators, trackers, benchmark reports, and interactive tools generally

> **Repo note**: this mode is currently thin in the library — a priority for the next `/library-add` pass (calculators, graders, branded data tools).

## What makes the mode work

- **Genuinely useful, on its own terms.** It has to earn its place in the workflow even if the brand vanished — usefulness is the whole ask.
- **Unique data or unique capability.** A commodity tool gets cloned; a tool built on data or a job only you can serve is defensible.
- **Every tool is a sensor.** Usage is a signal (who's in-market, what they need) wired back to the funnel — utility and data compound.
- **Low friction to first value.** The audience reaches something useful before any ask; the ask comes after the value, not before.

## Anti-patterns

- **Gated-utility greed**: burying the useful thing behind a form so early the value never lands.
- **Commodity tool**: a calculator anyone could build — copied within a quarter, no moat.
- **Utility-with-no-path**: a beloved free tool that never connects to the product or the funnel.
- **Neglect**: a tool shipped once and left to rot; utility that goes stale reads worse than no tool.

## When NOT to pick this mode

- You need a fast conversion in-window — pick **Direct-response**
- The job is to change a belief or get famous — pick **Aperture-setting** or **Attention / fame**
- You can't offer unique data or a real capability — a me-too tool isn't worth the build
- You can't commit to maintaining it — a decaying utility is a liability, not an asset
