## Big idea
Airbnb launched "Icons" — a curated set of fantastical, one-off experiential listings: sleeping in the actual house from Pixar's *Up* (floating with balloons), staying overnight at the X-Mansion, training with Kevin Hart, etc. Many were free or near-free to "win" via the app.

## Why it worked
- Reframed Airbnb from "rooms in houses" back to "once-in-a-lifetime experiences" after years of brand drift toward generic-rental-marketplace
- IP partnerships (Disney, Marvel, celebrity ecosystem) brought borrowed cultural relevance
- Spectacle of the launch (literally floating houses) made it news-worthy across non-marketing press
- Free/lottery model removed the price objection — created participation, not just admiration
- Re-staked Airbnb's position vs. hotels and Vrbo as the brand that does *interesting*

## What a CD should steal
- Spectacle as aperture: when the brand needs to reset perception, invest in undeniable, news-worthy executions
- IP partnerships that align with the brand's promise — borrowed cultural relevance from the right partners is force-multiplied
- Lottery / free participation removes the "I'd love to but can't afford it" objection
- Reframe via execution, not via tagline — Icons re-positioned Airbnb without anyone saying "we've re-positioned"

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Spectacle-without-substance: if the rest of the product experience doesn't ladder up, audience reverts to baseline perception
- IP-as-borrowed-equity: when the IP partner moves on or the cultural moment fades, the equity transfer reverses
- Hard to scale: Icons are by definition one-off; sustaining the platform requires constant new spectacle
- Distraction from core product: if Icons becomes the brand and core listings feel forgotten, customers churn
