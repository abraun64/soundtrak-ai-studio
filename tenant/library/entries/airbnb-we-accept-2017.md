## Big idea
"We Accept" was Airbnb's Super Bowl LI response to the 2017 US travel ban — a 30-second film of diverse faces with the line "We believe no matter who you are, where you're from, who you love, or who you worship, we all belong." The platform later evolved into "Made Possible by Hosts" — host-led, customer-photographed films celebrating real stays.

## Why it worked
- Real political context — the ad landed in a specific cultural moment that gave the brand position genuine stakes
- Aligned with Airbnb's structural business case (cross-cultural belonging IS the business model, not just a value)
- Host-led second act (Made Possible by Hosts) shifted the protagonist from Airbnb to the people who make it work — perfect identity-reinforcement
- Multi-year arc: aperture moment (We Accept) → identity-reinforcement platform (Made Possible by Hosts) → recovery during pandemic
- Real photography from real stays — earned credibility no studio shoot could match

## What a CD should steal
- Take a position aligned with the business model, not orthogonal to it — credibility compounds
- Cultural moment + brand stake = earned media; manufactured moments without stake fall flat
- Multi-phase arc: aperture moment → identity reinforcement platform → recovery — each phase informs the next
- Hosts/customers/users as protagonists — when the brand's product is people, the marketing should be too
- Real-photography aesthetic in a category dominated by stock — instant credibility

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Political-stake-without-business-fit: brand takes a position with no structural connection to its model — reads as opportunism
- One-moment-one-platform: aperture without follow-on platform; the cultural moment evaporates
- Host-led-as-filter: real hosts/customers in films require careful curation; tokenism gets called out
- Issue-fatigue: "We Accept" landed in 2017; the same play would feel tired in 2026 without evolution
