## Big idea
Always (the P&G feminine care brand) reframed the phrase "like a girl" — used culturally as an insult — into a statement of strength. A documentary-style hero film showed how the phrase loses its meaning between childhood and puberty, then invited girls to redefine it.

## Why it worked
- Reframed an everyday phrase the audience already had a reaction to — zero teaching required for the audience to feel the campaign
- Documentary craft made it feel like research, not advertising — earned editorial coverage
- Hashtag turned consumption into participation — girls and women shared their own "#LikeAGirl" actions
- Tied to the brand's category insight (puberty as a vulnerable identity moment) without being product-led
- In-school distribution component made it more than a media buy — became a programme

## What a CD should steal
- Reframing a phrase the audience already has emotional response to — instant resonance
- Documentary-as-ad: the format signals "research" and gets editorial treatment paid ads don't
- Couple the campaign to a programme (schools, partnerships) that makes the brand's commitment tangible
- The audience-state insight: spot a moment in your audience's life that the category quietly knows about and the world doesn't talk about

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Reframe-without-permission: brand without category authority taking on a cultural phrase reads as opportunistic
- Programme-as-veneer: announce school partnerships but never fund or sustain them
- Demographic-narrow without depth: campaign feels targeted at "girls" as a segment instead of treating the audience as people with a lived experience
