## Strategic spine
- **Audience insight**: people feel a vague unease about being tracked online, but it's invisible — so it feels free to give away and easy to ignore.
- **Big idea**: privacy = iPhone. Own the one uncontested, brand-true axis in a category where every phone's features have converged.
- **Key message**: what you do on iPhone stays on iPhone — you're in control of your data.
- **Product truth**: App Tracking Transparency, on-device processing, Mail Privacy Protection, Safari Intelligent Tracking Prevention, App Store privacy labels.
- **Channel mix**: hero films (emotion + attention) sitting on top of the `/privacy` page (the proof layer).

## Creative move
**What it did**: took an abstract, invisible benefit (privacy) and made it concrete, physical and funny by literalising the creepiness of data-tracking in the real world.

**The move** (portable):
- **Single-minded proposition** — one benefit, relentlessly. Not "iPhone does X, Y and Z" — just privacy, for years, under one end-line.
- **Dramatise the invisible** — make the abstract harm physical and absurd: the everyday act of being tracked, staged as something you can actually see happening to a person in the real world.
- **Humour as the disarming device** — exaggeration turns a dry, technical, easily-preachy topic into something entertaining and shareable.
- **Proof underneath the emotion** — the film sells the idea; the `/privacy` page makes it believable with named features. Emotion + evidence, layered.
- **Show the fix as the satisfying beat** — the moment the tracking is switched off and the intrusion vanishes = the product benefit made visceral.

**Why it worked**:
- In a feature-parity category you need ONE ownable axis; privacy was uncontested and true to Apple.
- Concreteness beats abstraction (behavioural science) — a staged, physical intrusion is *felt* where a statistic isn't.
- Humour lowers resistance and buys attention on a topic people would otherwise tune out.
- A consistent end-line over years compounds into ownership.

**When to steal**:
- You have ONE genuine differentiator in a me-too category → go single-minded, don't hedge into a feature list.
- The benefit is abstract/invisible (security, trust, compliance, "your data won't train someone else's model", uptime) → dramatise it physically.
- The subject is dry or worthy → humour/absurdity is the unlock, not gravitas.

**Anti-patterns / risks**:
- Single-minded only works if the claim is TRUE and defensible — the product must back it, or a bold SMP invites backlash.
- Humour that undercuts the credibility layer — you still need the proof.
- Smugness: privacy messaging trips into preachiness; the comedy is what keeps it likeable.
- Cloning "literalise the metaphor" with no real insight = empty gimmick.

## Visual direction
High-craft Apple film language — bright, characterful comedic set-pieces, recognisable pop-track needle-drops, and the stark **"Privacy. That's iPhone."** end-card. The `/privacy` landing page is clean-authoritative: generous whitespace, feature-by-feature proof, calm confident tone.
