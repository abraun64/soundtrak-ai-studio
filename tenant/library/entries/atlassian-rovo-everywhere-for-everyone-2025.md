## Strategic spine

**Audience insight**: Enterprise teams have institutional knowledge scattered across 30 SaaS tools — and the cost isn't the licensing, it's the lost context every time someone has to re-stitch what the company already knows.
**Big idea**: Rovo is your most legendary teammate — the one who actually knows everything the company knows and can act on it.
**Key message**: AI search + chat + agents that map billions of connections across your tools, so every answer feels tailored, contextual, and backed by your company's know-how.
**Product or service**: Atlassian Rovo (AI layer across Atlassian + connected enterprise apps — Confluence, Google Drive, Salesforce, Teams, Figma, ServiceNow, Miro, Github, Workday, etc.)
**Channel mix**: Owned video (LinkedIn organic primary surface), product-launch comms suite (likely paid + earned)

## Creative move

**What it did**: Launched Rovo as a horizontal AI layer across Atlassian + connected enterprise tools, positioned through a **teammate metaphor** ("your most legendary teammate") and a **visual system that deliberately fights the AI-launch aesthetic** — B&W documentary portraits + magazine-collage shapes + hand-drawn marks, instead of the standard "glowing orbs and abstract intelligence" template every other AI launch uses.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Metaphor as positioning** — "legendary teammate" carries connotations (the colleague who knows everyone, remembers everything, gets you unstuck) that feature lists can't.
- **Headline anchors on coverage + accessibility** — "everywhere, for everyone" addresses two real B2B-SaaS objections (limited surface area + role-restricted access) in five words.
- **Scale signalled by users, not capabilities** — 3M+ active users + "billions of connections"; adoption-as-proof.
- **Reframes AI value-prop from "answers" to "context"** — leans on the moat (your data + your wiring) not on the model.
- **Anti-AI-launch visual system** — fights the category convention. Real humans (B&W documentary portraits) + magazine-collage cutout shapes + hand-drawn squiggles are the *opposite* of glowing-blue-orb visual rhetoric. Communicates: this is a teammate, not an intelligence.
- **Rainbow-gradient as the AI-activation tell** — when the input field is "thinking," its border becomes a multicolor gradient. A single repeatable graphic cue that says "AI is doing work here" without text or icon clutter.
- **Multicolor pixel-painted product-surface icons** — Search / Chat / Agents each get a hand-crafted-feeling icon, not a sleek vector. Reinforces the craft-led-not-machine-led tone.

**Why it worked** (bullets):
- Teammate metaphor gives the buyer a frame to pitch Rovo internally without saying "agentic AI."
- Atlassian owns the connective tissue (Jira / Confluence / etc.), so "connects every app" is defensible, not aspirational.
- The visual system is *recognisably Atlassian* (house palette pushed hot) and *recognisably not generic AI* (B&W portraits + zine collage) — the brand stays itself while the category gets reframed.
- Adoption metric (3M+ users) defuses the "is this real?" question that haunts every AI launch.

**When to steal**:
- Launching a horizontal AI / agentic product where the category is fuzzy and the buyer can't picture daily use.
- When your product's moat is the data graph it sits on (not the model).
- When the category's default visual language (here: glowing-orb AI rhetoric) is unhelpfully homogeneous — fight it deliberately with a craft-led counter-aesthetic.
- When you need a single phrase that survives translation into the customer's own pitch to their internal stakeholders.

**Anti-patterns / risks**:
- The "teammate" metaphor only works when the product actually behaves like a teammate (proactive, contextual, remembers). Glorified search box + teammate framing = expectation gap.
- The collage / cutout aesthetic risks reading as "design school" if the underlying product UI isn't equally polished. Atlassian earns it because the product UI is sharp; on a clunky product the gap becomes the message.
- "Legendary" risks self-aggrandising on a smaller brand without Atlassian's category gravity.

## Visual direction

**Hero visual description**: Centered headline "Rovo is here" set in heavy black sans, with "is here" in lighter weight for emphasis hierarchy. Below the headline: B&W documentary photograph of two women (older glasses-wearing colleague + younger smiling colleague) looking at a laptop together — the photo is treated with cutout collage technique, layered against bold flat-color geometric shapes (blue + green wedges) with hand-drawn squiggle / spark marks around the figures. Top of frame: thick navy + electric-blue bar with three primary-colored dots (yellow, blue, green) and squiggle line motifs.

**Palette**: Atlassian house — navy + electric blue + green + white as the base; multicolor brights (yellow, orange, magenta, purple, red, lime) as percussion confetti shapes. Rainbow gradient (blue → purple → magenta → orange → green) reserved for AI-activation cues (input field active states). B&W photography deliberate against the saturated palette.

**Typography**: Atlassian's house family — Charlie Display / Inter Display variants. Heavy black weights for headlines, mixed-weight sub-emphasis ("Rovo **is here**" / "Search easier, **find faster**") for hierarchy. Body type cleanly geometric sans. Type set integrated INTO the composition, not floated above it.

**Photography / illustration style**: **B&W documentary photography + magazine-collage cutout treatment + hand-drawn line accents**. Real humans (not stock, not models — read as actual colleagues), shot in B&W to throw colour onto the brand graphics, then cut out and composited with flat-color geometric shapes behind/around. Hand-drawn squiggle lines, sparkle marks, dot patterns layered over. Reads as zine / craft / handmade — deliberately NOT machine-generated. Product UI screenshots in a parallel cleaner register (white space, real product, rainbow-gradient AI-active borders).

**Composition pattern**: Layered planes — photography + flat shapes + hand-drawn marks + type as 3–4 overlapping layers in brand-storytelling frames. Product-demo frames are cleaner: single focused crop, centered, white space generous. Two registers alternating across the campaign — story / product / story / product.

**Mood**: Friendly, energetic, confident, craft-led, human. The opposite of "powerful intelligence operating on your enterprise data." Specifically: warm-not-cold, handmade-not-machine, colleague-not-tool.
