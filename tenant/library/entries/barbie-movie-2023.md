## Big idea
The Barbie movie marketing claimed the colour pink for the summer of 2023. 100+ brand partnerships (Crocs, Bumble, Burger King, Microsoft, Airbnb's pink dream house, Gap, etc.) all amplified the colour. AI photo-generators let anyone create their "own Barbie box" portrait. Greta Gerwig's auteur film became unmissable cultural infrastructure.

## Why it worked
- Single colour ownership turned every pink object into ambient Barbie advertising — billions in earned-media-equivalent value
- AI Barbie-box generator gave audiences participation at zero cost — the meme spread the campaign for free
- Brand partnerships at unprecedented scale created the impression that the whole consumer economy was in on the moment
- Released same weekend as Oppenheimer ("Barbieheimer") — the timing accident became a cultural meme that doubled both films' opening weekends
- Mattel's strategic IP play: not just sell the film, but reposition Barbie as a culturally-relevant brand for adult women, fixing decades of brand drift
- Real physical executions (the life-size Barbie box installations, the pink Airbnb Malibu DreamHouse) generated paid press coverage no media buy could fund

## What a CD should steal
- Claim a colour, a season, a moment — not a tagline. Visual ownership scales better than verbal ownership.
- AI participatory mechanics at zero marginal cost — let the audience generate the content
- Cross-brand partnership saturation — when 100 brands amplify, no single brand has to fund the full reach
- Physical-scale executions for press coverage — earned beats paid on memorable physical builds
- Use a moment to reposition an old IP — film as brand-equity refresh, not just product launch

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Cross-brand saturation only works at this scale for cultural-juggernaut IP — most brands can't get 100 partners to play
- Colour ownership requires consistency — every partnership must respect the visual rules or the equity dilutes
- One-time-shot phenomena: this exact play cannot be repeated for the next Mattel IP without diminishing returns
- Co-dependence: Mattel and Warner Bros each took on risk that the other might underperform
