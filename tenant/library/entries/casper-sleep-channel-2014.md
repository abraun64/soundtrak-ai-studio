## Big idea
Casper saturated sleep-adjacent media: NYC subway cars (poetry-style OOH), podcasts (host-read ads that became a category meme), a Spotify "Sleep Channel" of ambient sounds, and a constant DR funnel ending in the 100-day-trial offer. Every touchpoint was conversion-tracked.

## Why it worked
- Owned a niche (sleep-adjacent media) where competitors weren't bidding — efficient CAC
- Podcast host-reads became culturally famous in their own right — "this episode brought to you by Casper" was a joke and a brand cue
- 100-day trial removed the highest-friction step of mattress buying (the irreversibility) — DR conversion lift
- Transit OOH used poetry/quirky design — content-like, not ad-like, made commuters opt into reading
- Every touchpoint was tracked and pruned — performance-marketing discipline applied to brand creative

## What a CD should steal
- Pick a media niche your competitors aren't bidding on — efficient CAC + cumulative brand presence
- Host-read podcast ads can become culture if the host has chemistry with the brand — pick partners, not slots
- Friction-removal as creative direction — what's the biggest "what if I hate it" objection? Engineer it out.
- Performance discipline + brand craft together: most teams treat them as opposing; the best teams compound them

## Anti-patterns / risks
- DR-without-brand-substance: pure performance marketing without distinctive creative becomes commodity — CAC creeps up
- Niche-media-discovery: as soon as competitors notice and bid up the niche, the arbitrage evaporates (Casper's podcast economics eroded by 2019)
- Trial-offer dependence: business model leans on trial conversion math; if return rates spike, the whole model wobbles
- Quirky-OOH-as-template: easy to imitate the surface (poetic transit ads) without the underlying DR discipline
