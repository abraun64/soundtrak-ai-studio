## Strategic spine

- **Audience insight**: B2B software founders/marketers whose positioning is muddy — and who know it, but can't fix it from the inside.
- **Big idea**: name the pain ("Let's fix your confusing positioning"), then prove the fix with a productised process and quantified client results.
- **Key message**: clear positioning → clearer homepage → measurable growth, in a focused two-week process.
- **Product**: positioning strategy + homepage rewrite + internal alignment deck.
- **Channel mix**: owned site as the conversion page + LinkedIn organic.

## Creative move

**What it did**: A consultancy homepage that leads with the buyer's pain in plain words, then de-risks a fuzzy, high-trust purchase with specifics and quantified proof.

**The move**:
- **Problem-first headline** that names the pain, not the service — "Let's fix your confusing positioning."
- A self-qualifying **"do you have a positioning problem?" checklist** before any pitch.
- **Radical specificity**: a week-by-week two-week sprint, pricing tiers by company size / ARR, "production-ready" deliverables.
- **Proof through quantified testimonials** ("+25% conversion," "+54% ARR"), customer logos surfaced early, real customer photos.
- **Modular, scannable sections** (deliverables · process · FAQ) — clarity as the meta-argument: they sell clarity, and the site *is* clarity.

**Why it worked**:
- Naming the pain earns the "that's me" nod before any claim — recognition beats assertion.
- Specificity de-risks a high-consideration purchase — the buyer can picture exactly what they get and what it costs.
- The medium proves the message: a positioning firm whose own positioning is razor-clear is its own case study.
- The clean / authoritative visual register signals competence to a design-literate B2B buyer.

**When to steal**:
- High-consideration B2B / pro-services where the buyer is unsure and needs de-risking.
- Any brief where **messaging clarity itself is the differentiator**.
- Founder-led / consultancy sites currently hiding behind jargon.

**Anti-patterns / risks**:
- Problem-first only works if you name the *buyer's* real pain, not a convenient strawman.
- Fixed pricing/scope exposes you — it only works if the offer is genuinely productised.
- Clean minimalism without real proof reads as empty; the quantified proof is what carries it.

## Visual direction

Clean / authoritative register ([[clean-authoritative]]): generous whitespace, left-aligned text with supporting imagery to the right, a restrained neutral palette + one accent, a modern readable sans, and customer logos + photo testimonials placed early. Restraint reads as confidence; the layout's clarity mirrors the messaging's clarity — form and argument are the same thing.
