## Big idea
Geico runs not one campaign but multiple parallel comedic platforms — the Gecko, the Cavemen, "Hump Day," "Did You Know," "It's What You Do," and many more — refreshed and rotated so the audience never feels the same ad twice. Every spot, regardless of platform, ends with the same DR proposition: "15 minutes could save you 15% or more."

## Why it worked
- Multiple platforms running in parallel = no fatigue; when one cools, another heats
- DR discipline preserved through every creative variation — the offer never disappears
- Cumulative brand equity: 25+ years of consistent CTA ("15 minutes / 15%") creates near-perfect category recall
- Comedy gives the otherwise-boring category permission to be remembered
- Brand acts like a stable of campaigns, not a single one — agency relationships and creative leadership rotated without breaking continuity
- Massive spend, but with measurable ROI per cohort — Geico is one of the most-tested advertisers

## What a CD should steal
- Parallel platforms reduce campaign fatigue and de-risk single-execution failure
- DR discipline can co-exist with brand craft — they're not opposing forces
- Repetition of the offer over decades creates near-perfect recall; consistency is the moat
- Categories that buyers find boring (insurance, taxes, utilities) reward comedic distinction disproportionately
- Plan for the long game — Geico's category position is the compound result of 25 years of consistent execution

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Spend-required floor: Geico's strategy needs massive media spend to sustain parallel platforms — most brands can't match
- DR-as-default: when the DR offer is too dominant, the brand work becomes a vehicle for the offer; creative-team morale and award recognition suffer
- Comedy ageing: spots from earlier eras now feel dated; long-running platforms need creative refresh investment
- Imitator drift: Progressive (Flo) and others have copied the multi-platform comedy model, eroding distinctiveness
