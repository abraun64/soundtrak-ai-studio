## Big idea
Gong (which records and analyses B2B sales calls) realised its product generated proprietary data nobody else had: what actually works in real sales conversations. Marketing turned that data into a relentless content engine — counter-intuitive findings, busted myths, charts, LinkedIn posts, podcasts (Reveal) — all backed by "we analysed X million calls."

## Why it worked
- Proprietary data is uncopyable content — competitors couldn't replicate the source, so couldn't replicate the authority
- Contrarian framing — "actually the top reps DON'T do X" — generates engagement on LinkedIn where consensus content dies
- LinkedIn-native format — short, chart-led, scroll-stopping — matched where the audience was
- Cumulative authority — every post added to Gong's claim of being *the* source of sales truth
- Pipeline impact: sales leaders pre-sold themselves before any rep called them; budget approval was easier

## What a CD should steal
- Data-as-content: if the product generates proprietary data, the data IS the campaign material
- Contrarian framing for engagement — agreement content is invisible on LinkedIn
- Platform-native craft — Gong's LinkedIn posts are made *for* LinkedIn, not adapted from blog posts
- Cumulative authority strategy — the 500th data post matters because the prior 499 built the credibility
- Proof-via-product flywheel — content drives trials, trials add more data, more data enables more content

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Data-content-without-data: many imitators show fake or thin "we analysed X" framings — audience smells it
- Contrarian-for-contrarian's-sake: when the finding isn't really surprising, the format becomes hollow
- Content treadmill: required cadence creates pressure that erodes quality over time
- Platform dependence: if LinkedIn changes its algorithm, the channel ROI shifts overnight
