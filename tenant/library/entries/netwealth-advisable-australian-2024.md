## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advisers + wealth professionals refining who they serve and how.
- **Insight**: advisers lack a shared, evidence-based language for their market.
- **Big idea**: a proprietary segmentation — four personas (Established/Emerging Affluent, Established/Emerging Mass) across six dimensions (Financial Capability, Resilience, Wellbeing, Advice Propensity, Tech Adoption, Brand Affinity) — that becomes the industry's vocabulary.
- **Channel**: white papers + reports + webinars + podcast + a self-serve segment-profiling tool.

## Creative move
**What it did**: packaged segmentation science into a returnable, named franchise.
**The move**:
- Own a *named lexicon* ("The Advisable Australian") that competitors end up borrowing.
- Pair the data with tactical playbooks (loyalty, value-gap) → utility, not just insight.
- A self-serve profiling tool turns passive research into an interactive touchpoint.
**Why it worked**: naming + framework = authority; recurring playbooks/tools = repeat engagement; segmentation is inherently adviser-useful.
**When to steal**: own a segmentation lexicon for your market; ship the tool + the playbook alongside the report, not just findings.
**Anti-patterns**: segmentation with no "so what" playbook; a one-off report instead of a multi-year franchise.
