## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 2: Aligned investment options**. Demonstrates how a financial product can use **identity alignment** + **values-led positioning** to convert ideology into deposits.

## The move

**What it does**: positions as a sustainable, socially conscious alternative to traditional banks. Customers can avoid fossil-fuel-related investments, donate a portion of fees to charitable causes, track personal sustainability scores. Tagline-level positioning: "climate-friendly banking."

**The move** (bullets):
- **Identity alignment as positioning core**: directly appeals to customers' environmental + social values — emotional connection beyond transaction
- **Personal sustainability score**: gamifies the ethical position into a personal metric (your impact, not the bank's)
- **Social proof + momentum**: highlights stories of customers taking sustainability steps → broad ethically-minded community → newcomers feel confident in choice
- **Identity = commitment**: when clients see ideals reflected in their financial activity, identity-as-ethical-investor strengthens and willingness to stay grows

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Identity alignment**: ideology → behaviour bridge built directly into the product
- **Social proof**: community of values-aligned customers signals safety in the choice
- **Reciprocity**: bank's charitable-fee donations create a feeling that the bank gives back, customer should stay

**When to steal**:
- B2B or B2C services where your audience has a strong values-driven identity (ethical investing, sustainable design, mission-driven consulting, B Corp clients)
- When you can convert your values position into a measurable user-facing metric (their score, their impact, their contribution)
- Professional services firms whose clients care about supply chain, ESG, social purpose

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't greenwash**: this strategy collapses brutally if the actual practices don't match the positioning. Audit before claiming.
- Don't add an "ethics layer" to a product that already serves the opposite — Aspiration was built ground-up for this segment
- The personal-score gamification requires real underlying data, not vanity metrics
