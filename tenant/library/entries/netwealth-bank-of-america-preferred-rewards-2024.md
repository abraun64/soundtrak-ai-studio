## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 11: Rewarding clients**. The lesson: **a multi-tier loyalty programme stacks three behavioural-science mechanisms** (reciprocity + gamification + loss aversion) to drive deepened engagement.

## The move

**What it does**: Bank of America's Preferred Rewards programme uses behavioural science to strengthen client loyalty through personalised rewards and incentives. Three mechanisms operating simultaneously:

**The move** (bullets):
- **Reciprocity engine**: offering discounts and benefits like lower fees → clients feel valued → fostering obligation to stay loyal
- **Gamification via tiered structure**: each tier (Gold / Platinum / Platinum Honors / etc.) is a level → encourages clients to take specific actions to progress → reinforces commitment to the bank as they level up
- **Loss aversion at the higher tiers**: exclusive benefits at higher levels → once a client achieves them, they're motivated to avoid losing them → strong retention lock-in
- **Threshold-based qualification**: typically asset-based (deposit + investment balance) → drives consolidation toward Bank of America from other institutions

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Reciprocity**: a benefit creates obligation
- **Gamification**: levels + progress + status create intrinsic motivation
- **Loss aversion (Kahneman)**: protecting what you've earned outperforms gaining what you could earn
- **Status quo bias**: once a client has consolidated assets to qualify for a tier, switching costs become prohibitive

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm with multi-product or multi-engagement clients where deeper consolidation could be rewarded
- Banks (obviously), but also: agency networks (multi-service rewards), accounting firms (multi-entity engagement discounts), legal firms (multi-matter pricing), consulting firms (multi-engagement preferred rates)
- When you can define meaningful tier thresholds that match real customer value depth — not arbitrary numbers
- When you can make the higher tiers genuinely scarce + valuable, not just "more of the same"

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't gamify trivial rewards** — if Platinum tier means "free monthly newsletter," the gamification collapses. Rewards must be material.
- Don't make tier qualification arbitrary — clients can tell when thresholds are designed to extract behaviour vs. recognise loyalty
- Don't make tier downgrade automatic and silent — sudden loss of status without warning kills trust. Clients should know the rules.
- Loyalty programmes can become commodities — Bank of America's works because they invest in actual differentiated benefits. Cheap clones don't.
