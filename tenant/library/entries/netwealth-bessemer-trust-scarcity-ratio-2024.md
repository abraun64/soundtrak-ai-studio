## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 9: Prompt and timely support**. Particularly instructive in the **UHNW / private client** end of professional services. The lesson: **a single resource-allocation ratio becomes a credibility anchor that competitors can't match**.

## The move

**What it does**: services multi-family offices, with a legacy stretching back to 1907. Combines storied history with high-touch personalised attention. As part of its communications, highlights its **3:1 client-to-employee ratio**, suggesting each client receives top-tier, individualised care.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Resource ratio as scarcity signal**: 3:1 (instead of 50:1 or 100:1 typical in mass-market wealth) is the operational proof of personalisation. Scarcity enhances perceived value.
- **Heritage anchor stacked on top**: "1907" carries gravitas competitors can't synthesise. Hard-to-fake authority.
- **Specific number, defensible**: 3:1 is verifiable and concrete — beats "high-touch" or "personalised service"
- **Two reinforcing anchors (history + ratio)**: clients can't tell which one is the load-bearing claim — they reinforce each other

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Scarcity**: limited availability per client = perceived value rises
- **Anchoring**: one dominant metric frames all subsequent judgments
- **Authority bias**: heritage + ratio = "they must be doing something right for 100+ years"
- **Operational transparency**: behind-the-scenes resource allocation made visible

**When to steal**:
- Any high-touch professional services firm whose model genuinely allocates resources differently than the category default
- Boutique law firms, family offices, executive coaching, senior consulting, niche specialist accounting
- When you have a quantifiable operational reality (low partner-to-associate ratios, low active-client counts per partner, dedicated team per client) that you can verify and own
- When heritage + a current operational metric can stack — but heritage alone is decoration, the operational claim is the substance

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't claim a ratio you can't sustain through growth** — Bessemer protects its ratio by being selective about new clients. A firm that brags 3:1 then scales to 50:1 loses the position permanently.
- Don't rely on heritage alone — "We've been around since 1907" without operational substance reads as resting on laurels
- Don't claim ratios you can't independently verify — clients (and competitors) will fact-check
