## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advisers, firm leaders, industry thinkers.
- **Insight**: trust in a platform is built by the people behind it, not by its features.
- **Big idea**: the joint-CEO (Matt Heine) personally hosts long-form conversations with the people advisers respect — so the platform shows up as an insider, not a vendor.
- **Channel**: podcast (Apple / Spotify / YouTube), ~monthly, 108+ episodes deep.

## Creative move
**What it did**: made the principal the host — credibility by association. The named tactic: **Brand as Founder** — the founder/CEO is the brand's voice and face.
**The move**:
- **Brand as Founder**: principal-as-host → insider status + relationship capital with the community.
- Long-form + no overt product promotion → trust over conversion.
- Guest range (Harvard academics, tech founders, top practice leaders) signals the brand's altitude.
**Why it worked**: 100+ episodes compound into authority; the host's access is the moat; restraint (no pitch) is what earns the audience.
**When to steal**: relationship-led B2B where the founder/principal has standing — host the conversation, don't sponsor it.
**Anti-patterns**: thinly-veiled product ads; inconsistent cadence (the compounding is the point); outsourcing the host away from the principal.
