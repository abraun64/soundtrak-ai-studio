## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 3: Guidance for the unexpected**. The lesson: **a written case-study library on your website**, organised by life-event scenarios, becomes the primary trust-building artifact for prospects considering whether you can help them through their specific situation.

## The move

**What it does**: Australian financial planning practice (operating since 2001) that uses written case studies to demonstrate how it helps clients through unexpected events. Topics covered include changing jobs, divorce, inheritance.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Life-event taxonomy as content architecture**: case studies organised by scenario the prospect is searching for (divorce, inheritance, job change) → SEO + emotional resonance both engaged
- **Specific over generic**: each case study addresses one named scenario in depth — prospects in that exact situation see themselves
- **Written depth over video glamour**: a substantive written narrative outperforms a 90-second video testimonial when prospects are researching a high-stakes decision
- **Long-tenure firm signal**: "since 2001" + multiple deep case studies = authority + persistence as standalone trust signal

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Loss aversion + authority bias engaged simultaneously**: prospects fearing a major loss find a firm with track record on exactly that scenario
- **Social proof, but specific**: not "lots of clients trust us" but "we helped someone like you through this exact thing"
- **Reduces uncertainty aversion**: pre-engagement, the case study lets the prospect rehearse what working with the firm looks like

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm where prospects' decision-brief is "can you help me through THIS specific thing?" — legal (M&A, family, succession), accounting (business sale, audit, divorce), consulting (turnarounds, exits), coaching
- When you have rich client outcomes you can anonymise and tell honestly
- When your audience googles "[specific scenario] help" before they google your category

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't write generic case studies that could apply to anyone — defeats the whole move. Specificity is the unlock.
- Don't dramatise; honesty + restraint + structure = trust. Tabloid-style "...and then everything went wrong" undermines.
- Don't ignore SEO — these pages are designed to rank for the scenarios prospects search
