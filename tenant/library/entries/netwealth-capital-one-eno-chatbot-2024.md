## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 9: Prompt and timely support**. The lesson: **giving the chatbot a name and a personality** (Eno) is the difference between "customer service automation" (cost-cutting language) and "always-on assistant" (loyalty language).

## The move

**What it does**: Capital One launched **Eno**, a chatbot providing customers with real-time information about account balance, transactions, and credit score. Eno also lets customers pay bills, check rewards, monitor credit usage. Uses AI to understand requests and respond conversationally.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Named, personified chatbot**: Eno isn't "Customer Service AI" — it has a name, a tone, a persona. Customers refer to "Eno" → it becomes a relationship-bearing entity, not infrastructure
- **24/7 availability as the loyalty mechanism**: financial questions arise at 11pm; Eno answers. Loyalty driver: clients don't switch to a bank where the answer waits until business hours.
- **Action-enabled, not just information-enabled**: pay bills, check rewards — Eno isn't a FAQ bot, it transacts
- **AI conversational tone**: customer expectations of natural language interaction are now defaults, not differentiators

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Immediacy**: real-time responses kill the friction of "I'll deal with this later" present-bias
- **Cognitive ease**: conversational interface lower-effort than navigating menus
- **Reciprocity**: Capital One being available at all hours creates a sense of being valued → return loyalty

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm whose clients have routine information requests that don't require expert judgment — "what's my balance / status / next step"
- Legal (case status, document status), accounting (return status, lodgement reminders), consulting (project status), agency (campaign metrics), coaching (session scheduling)
- When you can name + personify the assistant → it becomes part of the brand, not part of the cost structure
- When you can route routine inquiries through the bot AND escalate gracefully to humans for non-routine

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't deploy a chatbot that can only handle 30% of inquiries** — clients abandon and never trust again. Better to release a high-precision narrow scope first, expand later.
- Don't make the persona too forced ("Hi! I'm Eno and I'm SO excited to help!") — over-friendly bots read as juvenile
- Don't hide the escalation path — clients need to know "talk to a human" is one tap away
- Don't market the chatbot as "AI-powered" if AI is decoration — clients can tell
