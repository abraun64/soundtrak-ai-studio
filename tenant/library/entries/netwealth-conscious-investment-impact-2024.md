## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 2: Aligned investment options**. The annual impact report is the **load-bearing artifact** — it converts the firm's values position into structured, audit-quality outcome data clients can use to justify the relationship to themselves and their stakeholders.

## The move

**What it does**: founded 2019, vision to unlock mainstream investment markets to fund assets that create positive social/environmental impact. Funds invest in specialist disability accommodation, social/affordable housing, distributed energy, social impact bonds. Annual impact report details both financial performance + impact metrics across three focus areas (Environment & Climate, Health & Education, Social Infrastructure).

**The move** (bullets):
- **Annual impact report as primary trust artifact**: a single dense document carries the values claim with audit-grade specificity (MwH generated, dwellings financed, children supported, tonnes of CO2 offset)
- **Three-axis impact framework**: focuses scoring/reporting on Environment+Climate / Health+Education / Social Infrastructure — gives clients a vocabulary for talking about their portfolio's impact
- **Impact Management Norms (IMN) scoring**: external structured methodology (0-5 scale: Act-to-avoid-harm → Benefit-stakeholders → Contribute-to-solutions) provides third-party-style rigour
- **B Corp certification**: third-party endorsement → authority bias engaged

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Operational transparency**: the impact report literally shows behind-the-scenes effort + outcomes → perceived value rises
- **Identity alignment + social proof**: clients see their capital aggregated into a community delivering measurable impact
- **Authority bias**: B Corp + IMN scoring = third-party rigour beyond marketing claims

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm where your clients want to **justify your fees to others** (their board, their family, their compliance team) — give them an artifact built for that
- Impact investing, ESG-positioned funds, mission-driven consulting, social enterprise services
- When you can build a single dense annual artifact that consolidates a year's value delivery

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't publish an "impact report" that's a brochure dressed up — clients can tell. The structured metrics + the third-party framework are what makes this defensible.
- Vanity metrics ("X clients served") aren't impact metrics — outcome data is
