## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as a **cross-industry** exemplar for **Value Driver 1: Goal-based advice**. The lesson: behaviour-change products can use gamification to convert distant goals into immediate-feedback loops — directly portable to financial advice, professional development, health, and anywhere clients need to maintain consistent engagement against long-horizon outcomes.

## The move

**What it does**: language-learning platform that turns lessons into fun, bite-sized challenges. Helps learners adopt a consistent study habit — vital for long-term success.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Gamification**: badges, points, levels transform practice into a game → boosts motivation, encourages daily use
- **Streak mechanic as commitment device**: the "streak" nudges learners to log in daily, reinforcing commitment-to-keep-learning. Loss aversion of breaking the streak is sometimes stronger than the gain of learning.
- **Immediate feedback**: quick corrections + progress updates tap into desire for instant gratification → users stay aware of improvement and maintain momentum

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Gamification + habit formation**: small daily rewards create a virtuous cycle of positive habit
- **Goal gradient effect**: as users get closer to streak milestones, they exert more effort
- **Immediacy**: feedback loops fight present-bias by surfacing micro-rewards inside long-horizon goals

**When to steal**:
- Any client-engagement product where the goal is years away but daily/weekly micro-engagement matters
- Financial planning apps, coaching platforms, professional-development programmes, health/wellness services
- When you can break a long-term goal into checkable daily/weekly micro-actions with feedback

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't gamify the outcome (badges for "hitting your retirement number" feel hollow when achieved). Gamify the **behaviour** that compounds toward the outcome.
- Don't use streaks if breaking them is genuinely costly to the client (e.g. a client on holiday isn't a failure)
