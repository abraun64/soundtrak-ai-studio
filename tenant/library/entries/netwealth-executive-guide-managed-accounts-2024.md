## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advisers + practice owners weighing managed-account solutions.
- **Insight**: a "product" is easy to defer; a "business transformation" demands attention now.
- **Big idea**: a hub of 14+ resources that frames managed accounts as a strategic business move — aspirational *and* achievable — not a feature.
- **Channel**: themed resource hub — white papers + expert voices + practitioner case studies + beginner guides.
- **Ran**: a ~3-month multi-step campaign (time-boxed burst, not an always-on hub).

## Creative move
**What it did**: elevated a product category into a strategy narrative. The named tactic: **product-category education** — educate the market on the *category* (not a product) to create demand and own the authority.
**The move**:
- Reframe the category as business transformation, not a feature set.
- Pair thought-leadership with practitioner case studies (incl. a Acme Co story) → aspirational *and* proven.
- A beginner-to-advanced spread reduces implementation anxiety.
**Why it worked**: strategy framing earns attention a product page can't; real practitioner stories de-risk the leap.
**When to steal**: own a product category as a strategic narrative — lead with transformation + proof, not specs.
**Anti-patterns**: a feature-led product page wearing a "guide" label; aspiration with no practitioner proof.
