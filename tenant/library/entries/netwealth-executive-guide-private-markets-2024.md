## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advisers implementing or expanding a private-markets offer.
- **Insight**: complex categories create uncertainty; the brand that reduces it earns the relationship.
- **Big idea**: a tiered guide — Unlock → Build your value prop → Advanced playbook — that meets advisers at any knowledge level.
- **Channel**: guide hub + three progression-based special reports + supporting articles + a podcast.
- **Ran**: a ~3-month multi-step campaign (time-boxed burst, not an always-on hub).

## Creative move
**What it did**: owned a hard category by structuring the learning, not pushing the product. The named tactic: **product-category education** — educate the market on the *category* (not a product) to create demand and own the authority.
**The move**:
- Three progression tiers (foundational → implementation → advanced) → one asset serves novices and experts.
- Reduces adviser uncertainty on a complex asset class → trust → lead-gen.
- Platform offering integrated, but downstream of the education.
**Why it worked**: authority on the hard stuff is the differentiator; tiering widens the addressable audience; "guide" framing signals help, not sell.
**When to steal**: a complex/intimidating category you want to own — build a tiered guide and lead with education.
**Anti-patterns**: a flat one-level guide (loses novices or experts); an overt product push wearing a "guide" label.
