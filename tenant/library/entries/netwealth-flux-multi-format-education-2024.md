## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 8: Knowledge for empowerment**. **Flux is Netwealth's own subsidiary** — making this a case study the operator has direct operational visibility into. The lesson: **multi-format content** (app + podcast + newsletter + courses + tools) outperforms single-channel education for younger segments who consume on the channel-of-the-moment.

## The move

**What it does**: provides Emerging Affluent clients (Gen Y / Gen Z) with financial education via mobile app + podcast + digital newsletter. Weaves together education, immediate feedback, and competitive elements.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Gamified digital courses**: bite-sized digital courses on personal finance topics (investing, super, budgeting, home loans) → uses gamification + habit formation + leaderboards + prize incentives
- **Micro-learning + quizzes**: regular stream of short podcasts, videos, written content (ETFs, super mechanics, responsible spending, "money hacks", business news) → quick wins aligned with progress principle. Immediate feedback on quiz answers keeps motivation high.
- **Personal budgeting via Open Banking**: users connect bank accounts to view financial position, income, expenses. Detailed expense breakdown, subscription alerts, period comparison, projections.
- **Financial product references**: users assess relevant products (term deposits, bank accounts) → reduces uncertainty, often barrier to action

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Gamification + habit formation**: small rewards create virtuous cycles
- **Progress principle**: immediate feedback on correct/incorrect answers reinforces new knowledge
- **Cognitive ease via micro-format**: bite-sized content fits available attention
- **Authority transfer**: Netwealth's institutional credibility flows through to Flux as the consumer-facing brand

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm whose future clients are too young to be served by current offerings — but you want them in the pipeline now
- When you can build content surfaces matched to where the segment lives (TikTok, Spotify, Instagram, app)
- When you can structure content as micro-learning with feedback loops, not heavy reads
- When you can productise into a subsidiary brand without diluting the parent's positioning

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't make the subsidiary brand "feel like the parent's youth wing"** — Flux is genuinely young-led-looking. Half-hearted versions read as parents-trying-to-look-cool.
- Don't make every quiz/course feel like a sales funnel — the content has to earn its keep as content
- Don't ignore the channel — podcasts on Spotify, newsletters via email, courses in the app. Right content, right place.
