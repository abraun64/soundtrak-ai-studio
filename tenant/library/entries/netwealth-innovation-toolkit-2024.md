## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advice-firm teams + leaders who want to innovate but lack a method.
- **Insight**: "innovation" feels abstract; teams need do-it-now scaffolding, not theory.
- **Big idea**: ship usable IP — a toolkit of facilitated exercises (Customer Journey Workshop, AdviceTech Roadmap, a 35-card Innovation deck, prioritisation poster) firms run themselves.
- **Channel**: downloadable templates + supporting articles + a CPD keynote ("Innovation starts with curiosity").

## Creative move
**What it did**: replaced a thought-leadership report with a hands-on toolkit.
**The move**:
- Deliver *usable* IP (workshops, card deck, posters) — the brand rides along every time a team runs one.
- Low barrier to entry ("sticky notes, markers, curiosity") + explicitly shareable / hackable.
- A report + keynote frame the "why" so the tools have a narrative.
**Why it worked**: a tool gets used (and re-used); use = repeated brand association + loyalty; adaptability beats a locked-down asset.
**When to steal**: when you can package your method as a do-it-yourself kit, not just a PDF — make it adaptable on purpose.
**Anti-patterns**: a "toolkit" too branded/rigid to actually use; tools shipped with no facilitation guidance.
