## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 1: Goal-based advice**. Demonstrates how software can tie financial decisions to "what matters" in a client's life — shifting focus from short-term thinking toward long-term aspirations.

## The move

**What it does**: SaaS platform that lets advisers + clients co-create plans rooted in **personal values**, not just financial returns. Clients see money as a means to fulfilling life goals rather than an end in itself.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Values-based discovery**: illuminates personal values upfront, so the plan is built on what the client actually cares about
- **Co-creation as commitment device**: clients and advisers jointly set priorities and goals → shared ownership drives stickiness
- **Real-time adaptability**: plans can be updated on the fly, so they stay relevant as life shifts (and clients stay engaged with the live document)

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Temporal discounting countered**: by making future goals concrete and value-anchored, the future-reward becomes immediate emotionally
- **Identity alignment**: surfacing personal values up front anchors the plan to the client's self-concept, increasing commitment
- **Anticipatory utility**: seeing the plan = pleasure from anticipating the future, drives engagement before any outcome lands

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm whose engagement involves long-horizon planning (legal succession, business strategy, exec coaching, financial advice)
- When the client decision is rooted in life context, not just functional outcome
- When "co-creation" can be productised as a recurring interaction model, not a one-off discovery workshop

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't bolt "values" onto an existing functional tool as a tick-box exercise — must shape the entire interaction model
- Don't make the platform read like compliance documentation; clients have to want to come back
