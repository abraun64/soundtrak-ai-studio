## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 9: Prompt and timely support**. The lesson: **AI deployed inside the firm to make staff omniscient** is the highest-leverage application of generative AI for professional services — clients get faster, more accurate answers without the firm hiring more specialists.

## The move

**What it does**: Morgan Stanley developed a knowledge base — **AskResearchGPT** — for its staff. Features hundreds of thousands of digitised documents and webpages: Morgan Stanley research on 2000+ companies, procedure documents, quick reference product cards. All in a secure, private location.

Using OpenAI's technology, the company created a chatbot for staff that dives into this repository to answer questions like:
- "How can a client set up a Morgan Stanley online account?"
- "What is the house view on Apple?"
- "How should a client disperse of a Monet painting they just found in their attic?"

**Jeff McMillan** (responsible for big data and AI at Morgan Stanley): *"You essentially have the knowledge of the most knowledgeable person in wealth management instantly. Think of it as having our Chief Investment Strategist, Chief Global Economist, Global Equities Strategist, and every other analyst around the globe on call for every adviser, every day."*

**The move** (bullets):
- **Internal-facing AI as the highest-leverage deployment**: clients benefit indirectly through faster + better adviser responses — but the surface area of risk is contained (staff oversight before answers reach clients)
- **Repository as the moat**: 100,000s of internal documents + Morgan Stanley research on 2000+ companies = the model has institutional context generic models don't
- **Conversational query interface**: advisers ask in natural language → the friction of "I don't know where to look for this" disappears
- **Effectively cloning your best people**: every adviser has access to the firm's collective IQ, not just their own + Google
- **Indirect client benefit, direct loyalty impact**: clients experience advisers who suddenly know everything → trust + response quality both rise

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Authority bias**: client perceives adviser-with-AI as more competent than adviser-without
- **Immediacy**: questions answered in seconds, not days
- **Cognitive ease for adviser**: lower internal lookup cost = higher willingness to answer client questions thoroughly

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm with significant internal IP (research, methodology, precedent, case law, prior engagements) that staff currently can't efficiently access
- Legal (case law, precedent search, internal memos), accounting (rulings, position papers, prior engagement files), consulting (frameworks, prior project IP), agency (creative archives, brand books, past briefs)
- When the firm's competitive advantage is "we've seen this before" and you want to make that accessible to junior staff in real time
- When the security/compliance constraints can be solved with secure deployment + role-based access

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't deploy on top of bad source data** — AI surfacing outdated/incorrect internal documents amplifies errors at scale
- Don't go client-facing before staff-facing — internal deployment is the right first step. Client-facing AI is a different risk profile.
- Don't replace expert judgment with AI output — AskResearchGPT informs the adviser's answer, doesn't deliver it directly to clients
