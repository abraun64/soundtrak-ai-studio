## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 6: Communicate often, proactively**. The lesson: at scale, AI can enable advisers to send **customised** (not generic) proactive comms at speed — turning what used to be a luxury (the high-touch personal email when something happens) into a default behaviour.

## The move

**What it does**: AI-enabled system used by ~90% of Morgan Stanley's ~16,000 advisers at least monthly. Every day, an adviser logs in and is presented with a buffet of information and actions to take with their clients. Complex system based on:

- Client behaviours (phone, email, text interactions)
- Information from advice documents (goals, financial model, investment plans)
- External info (market news)
- AI + machine learning overlayed to produce advice alerts, investment ideas, life event notifications

**Example from source**: if the market drops significantly, advisers can hit one button to send an automated-yet-customised email to every client. System fills in Name and percentages automatically: *"Hello [Client Name], I just want to let know the market was down by X%, and your portfolio was down by X%, due to your (e.g.) 60/40 split. Based on my review of your financial plan (which was updated last month), you're currently still around a X% probability of success, which is where we want to be. You can access the plan by clicking on the link."*

**The move** (bullets):
- **AI as adviser amplifier, not adviser replacement**: the human adviser still sends the email; the AI does the lookup + drafting work that used to take 20 minutes per client
- **Behavioural + transactional + market data fused**: trigger comes from market event, content draws on client's specific portfolio + plan
- **Customisation at scale**: 16,000 advisers × thousands of clients each × multiple touchpoints = behaviour change at industry scale, not pilot scope
- **Daily login as habit-forming UI**: the system creates a daily ritual for advisers — they come for the actions, stay for the behaviour change

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Uncertainty reduction**: clients receiving a customised email during a market drop feel held, not abandoned
- **Reciprocity**: the proactive touch creates obligation toward the adviser
- **Authority bias + operational transparency**: client sees adviser's awareness of their specific situation in real-time

**When to steal**:
- Any large-scale professional services firm whose loyalty risk increases during market/regulatory/external-event shocks
- When you have the data infrastructure (or can build it) to fuse client-specific data with external triggers
- When advisers/professionals are the constraint on proactive comms, not technology
- Smaller firms can adopt simplified versions — CRM templates triggered by external events, even without ML

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't fully automate the send** — adviser oversight is what makes the email feel personal. Pure AI-sent comms read as spam.
- Don't trigger too often — uncertainty reduction comms only work if they're reserved for actual uncertainty moments
- Don't let the template show — clients should never feel they got the same email everyone got
