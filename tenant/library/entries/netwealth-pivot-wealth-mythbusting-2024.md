## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 4: Innovative advice**. The lesson: **challenging conventional wisdom** (via newsletter content) doubles as a novelty hook + authority signal — capturing attention while positioning the firm as someone who sees what others miss.

## The move

**What it does**: Pivot Wealth (founded by Ben Nash) challenges conventional financial wisdom — for example, the standard "pay down your mortgage ASAP" advice — by revealing hidden drawbacks like higher tax bills or missed investment opportunities. Counter-intuitive perspective captures attention and showcases authority.

Example shown in source: an email opening with "Property upgraders beware… The conventional wisdom says that paying down your mortgage is the BEST thing to do with your money, but for a lot of people this wisdom isn't that wise. One client recently fell into this trap and ended up paying $20k p.a. MORE tax than they should have."

**The move** (bullets):
- **Counter-intuitive hook as the open**: lead with the contrarian claim → reader's "wait, what?" reflex carries them past the first line
- **Specific client example as proof**: $20k/year tax overpayment is concrete and shocking → not theoretical
- **Founder-as-character voice**: Ben Nash signs the emails personally → reader feels they're hearing from a person who'd say controversial things in private
- **Educational depth as authority**: each newsletter teaches one move the reader didn't know existed → novelty effect + authority bias both engaged

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Novelty effect**: fresh stimuli trigger excitement and engagement — strong for Newcomers + Settled clients who haven't seen these tactics elsewhere
- **Authority bias**: the contrarian-but-substantiated take positions the firm as "actually knows things others don't"
- **Loss aversion engaged**: the $20k example makes the reader fear they might be making the same mistake

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm whose category has a body of "received wisdom" that's actually wrong or incomplete in common situations
- Tax / accounting (received wisdom: "always claim the maximum deductions"), legal (received wisdom: "always negotiate harder"), consulting, coaching
- When you have an opinionated founder/principal whose voice can carry founder-led-content cleanly
- When your audience is reading newsletters and a single counter-intuitive insight can earn a long-term subscription

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't be contrarian without being right**: the move dies if the conventional wisdom turns out to be conventional for good reason in your example. Pick battles you can win.
- Don't sustain the same myth-busting tone for years — eventually you become the new conventional wisdom. Rotate or evolve.
- Don't lead every newsletter with myth-busting; the move needs space between to land
