## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as a **cross-industry** exemplar for **Value Driver 1: Goal-based advice**. The lesson: high-trust intangible-outcome services (health, like wealth) win loyalty by personalising the path to the outcome rather than dictating it.

## The move

**What it does**: digital weight-loss programme using behavioural science to help people form lasting, personalised health habits. Doesn't dictate a diet — focuses on the individual's unique goals, values, and lifestyle (directly mirrors goal-based wealth advice).

**The move** (bullets):
- **Tailored guidance**: registered nutritionists + coaches create custom action plans per participant — service is personalised at the human layer, not just the algorithm
- **Co-creation**: users set their own health goals → commitment + consistency from active ownership
- **Adaptive support**: interactive tools (articles, recipes, progress tracking) adjust to evolving needs — same logic as a wealth manager updating advice as circumstances change
- **Clinical credibility**: endorsed by UK's NHS + reports 9-in-10 success rate → social proof + authority bias both engaged

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Identity alignment**: programme reflects user's lifestyle and values, not a generic prescription
- **Self-determination theory**: competence (tailored plan) + autonomy (user-set goals) = sustained motivation
- **Authority bias**: NHS endorsement is the load-bearing trust signal in a category drowning in pseudoscience

**When to steal**:
- High-trust intangible-outcome services where the prescription must match the person, not the protocol
- Wealth management, coaching, legal succession planning, executive search, consulting engagements
- When you can pair human expertise (coach) with digital scale (app) — the hybrid model is the move

**Anti-patterns**:
- Don't claim "personalised" without staffing actual humans to deliver it — the difference between a real coach and a chatbot is obvious to clients
- Don't lead with the clinical/regulatory endorsement; lead with the personalisation. Endorsement is the deciding trust signal at the end, not the opening hook.
