## Strategic spine
- **Audience**: advisers + wealth professionals.
- **Insight**: advisers tire of "tech for tech's sake"; culture/behaviour is the real driver of what they must adopt.
- **Big idea**: position *culture* as the primary driver of technology adoption — five mega-trends (trust deficit / sharing economy, values-driven "help me feel good", gender neutrality / female empowerment, expectation economy, spiritual / wellbeing).
- **Channel**: white paper + five deep-dive articles (each its own landing page) + video.
- **Ran**: a ~3-month multi-step campaign (time-boxed burst).

## Creative move
**What it did**: reframed a category conversation away from features.
**The move**:
- Lead with a cultural / behavioural lens → advisers feel strategically guided, not sold.
- Each trend gets its own deep-dive landing page → SEO breadth + multiple entry points.
- Tech framed as a *response* to culture → the platform becomes the obvious answer.
**Why it worked**: reframing wins where feature-comparison loses; "why" content earns attention + altitude.
**When to steal**: reframe a category conversation with a cultural / behavioural lens before you ever mention the product.
**Anti-patterns**: trend-spotting that never links back to the buyer's decision; culture as decoration on a product pitch.
