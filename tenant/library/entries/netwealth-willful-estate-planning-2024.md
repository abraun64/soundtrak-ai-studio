## Why this is in the library

Cited in the operator's Netwealth IQ Report *Creating Loyalty That Lasts* as an exemplar for **Value Driver 3: Guidance for the unexpected**. Particularly relevant for **legal services** — estate planning has been the category most associated with procrastination + anxiety, and Willful's simplification-led approach is a template for legal-services productisation.

## The move

**What it does**: online platform simplifying estate planning. Helps people overcome procrastination and anxiety around the unexpected (death, incapacitation). Provides last will, testament, power of attorney through a friction-reduced UX.

**The move** (bullets):
- **Loss aversion as motivator (handled honestly)**: addresses users' fear of leaving loved ones unprotected directly — without dramatising. Provides a straightforward path to securing the future.
- **Simplification of intimidating process**: user-friendly interface + engaging language reduces cognitive load. Makes the previously-overwhelming feel manageable.
- **Productised legal service**: a service that traditionally required a solicitor visit becomes a 30-minute online flow. Category-defining unbundling.

**Why it works** (behavioural science):
- **Loss aversion**: surfaces the cost of inaction (loved ones unprotected) → drives action that otherwise gets perpetually deferred
- **Cognitive ease**: friction-reduction in copy + UX overcomes the "I'll deal with it later" present-bias
- **Reducing ambiguity aversion**: clearly mapped process eliminates the "I don't even know what's involved" objection

**When to steal**:
- Any professional services firm where the highest-friction barrier is **the prospect's anxiety about the process itself**, not the cost
- Legal services (wills, conveyancing, simple corporate matters), accounting (BAS, tax returns), financial planning (super consolidation, insurance)
- When your category traditionally requires an in-person meeting and unbundling is overdue

**Anti-patterns**:
- **Don't unbundle complexity that legitimately requires expert judgement** — wills are productisable; estate-planning for a $50M trust isn't. Know the line.
- Don't strip out reassurance copy to look "minimal" — anxiety-laden categories need the friendly tone as much as the simple flow
