## Big idea
Paddy Power built its brand on relentless cultural mischief — wading into political moments others avoided (running a billboard saying "Throw your money down the drain" in 2010), creating betting markets on absurd things (will the Pope wear a Manchester United scarf), sponsoring the wrong people, sponsoring causes for laughs. Every quarter brought another small audacious stunt.

## Why it worked
- Earned media became the marketing channel — newsworthy stunts cost a fraction of paid equivalents
- Tone gave the audience social ammo — sharing a Paddy Power stunt is itself a personality signal
- Continual cadence kept the brand top-of-mind without traditional always-on advertising
- Regulated category turned to its advantage — betting brands face advertising restrictions, so earned media became the workaround
- Permission to provoke was built incrementally — each stunt expanded the licence for the next
- Internal culture (small, fast, brave) made the cadence possible; bigger competitors couldn't match the speed

## What a CD should steal
- Earned-media-engine as the spine of the marketing motion — cheaper, distinctive, scales
- Cadence matters more than any single stunt — the platform is the relentlessness, not one execution
- Audience-as-amplifier — when sharing the brand signals taste/personality, the audience does the distribution
- Provocateur positioning requires escalation — each stunt has to advance the licence, not just repeat it
- Regulated categories can use the constraint as creative direction

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Mischief-without-judgment: one badly-judged stunt can erase years of equity (Paddy Power has had multiple near-misses)
- Provocateur fatigue: audiences tire of relentless cleverness — the brand needs sincerity moments to balance
- Imitator dilution: every brand trying to be the mischief brand crowds the position; first-mover advantage is finite
- Regulatory risk: regulators eventually catch up; the workaround channels get restricted
- Culture-dependent: requires an internal culture that can move at speed and absorb occasional misses
