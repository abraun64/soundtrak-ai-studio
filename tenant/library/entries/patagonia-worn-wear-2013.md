## Big idea
Patagonia turned used Patagonia into a more authentic version of the brand. Worn Wear is a repair programme, a used-gear marketplace, a tour bringing repair stations to communities, and a series of films of customers telling stories about specific battered jackets and packs.

## Why it worked
- Reframed "used" as more authentic, not less — clever inversion of the consumption norm
- Customer-as-protagonist: real people, real gear, real stories — credibility unscripted
- Programme + content + commerce in one motion — repair stations create stories; films distribute stories; marketplace monetises
- Reinforced existing-customer identity ("real Patagonia people repair") and created a tier-of-belonging for prospects
- Backed the "Don't Buy This Jacket" stance with infrastructure — credibility of action

## What a CD should steal
- Reframe a category negative ("used = lesser") into a category positive
- Build a programme, not a campaign — programmes generate content forever, campaigns expire
- Use customer stories as primary content — the specificity of "this jacket, this trip, 1996" beats any voiceover
- Pair the stance with the infrastructure that proves it (repair stations, marketplace) — credibility compounds

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Identity-reinforcement-without-operational-backing: brand without actual circular infrastructure attempting this — empty
- Customer-story-as-template: scripted, polished "real customer" stories read fake; the rough edges matter
- Greenwash trap: any sustainability play without operational change is liability not asset
