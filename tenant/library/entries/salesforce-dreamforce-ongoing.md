## Big idea
Dreamforce is Salesforce's annual conference, but its real product is identity formation for the Salesforce ecosystem — admins, developers, partners, customers. The event combines product announcements, mass keynotes, training tracks, celebrity programming (U2, Will.i.am, the Dalai Lama have appeared), and ecosystem expo into a multi-day cultural moment in San Francisco.

## Why it worked
- Reframed the buyer's identity: "I use Salesforce" became "I am a Salesforce person" (Trailblazer, Admin, Architect)
- Participatory in a way most B2B marketing isn't — attendees do work, network, share, post
- Created a content engine that ran 51 weeks a year ("road to Dreamforce" content, post-event content)
- Pipeline impact via ecosystem stickiness — Trailblazers stay, evangelise, and influence buying committees at their next employer
- Status currency — "I'm going to Dreamforce" is professional signal, not just attendance
- Pre-empted the category — competitors holding their own conferences are perpetually compared to Dreamforce

## What a CD should steal
- B2B participation works when the participation forms professional identity, not just attendance
- Build a content engine around the moment, not just during it — 51 weeks of road-to + post-event
- Status currency over feature pitching — attendees should feel professionally elevated by being there
- Celebrity programming as cross-audience bridge — brings non-Salesforce people in to see the ecosystem at scale
- Multi-year platform compounding — Dreamforce's value is partly that it's been Dreamforce for 20+ years

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Conference-as-pipeline-only: events optimised purely for sales meetings hollow out the cultural moment
- Celebrity-as-distraction: high-profile names that don't connect to the audience's identity become awkward
- Cost ceiling: Dreamforce's economics work for Salesforce-scale brands; smaller B2B conferences shouldn't try to copy the spectacle
- Identity dependence: if the ecosystem starts feeling cult-like or insular, recruiting new members gets harder
