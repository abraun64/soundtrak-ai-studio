## Big idea
Sephora elevated its loyalty programme beyond points-for-purchases by tiering membership (Insider → VIB → Rouge), opening a community forum where members exchange reviews and tutorials, and giving meaningful birthday gifts plus members-only experiences. The programme creates an identity tier in the audience's life, not just a discount.

## Why it worked
- Tiered membership creates aspiration upward and identity downward — Rouge members brag; new members aim to upgrade
- Community forum drives organic content and peer-validated reviews — credibility paid couldn't match
- Birthday gifts and member-only experiences are emotional, not transactional — rewards shape behaviour beyond purchases
- Data feeds back: member behaviour informs product curation, which improves retention
- Retail-D2C bridge: programme works in-store, online, and via app; physical and digital reinforce each other
- Cross-brand programme: members earn points across the whole Sephora catalogue, locking them in across the multi-brand space

## What a CD should steal
- Loyalty programme as identity tier, not just points — tiered membership creates aspirational dynamics
- Community forum as content engine and credibility source
- Emotional rewards (birthday gifts, exclusive experiences) shift behaviour more durably than transactional ones
- Cross-brand programme structure locks in audience across the catalogue
- Data flywheel: member behaviour → product curation → retention → more data

## Anti-patterns / risks
- Points-only programme: discount cards with no identity dimension — competitors copy in a week
- Tier inflation: when "VIB" status becomes too easy to reach, the aspirational element collapses
- Community moderation: forums need active moderation; abandoned forums become a credibility liability
- Data-privacy backlash: programmes increasingly need to justify data collection to wary audiences
