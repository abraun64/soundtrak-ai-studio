Creating Loyalty That Lasts

Authored by the operator, originally published as Netwealth IQ Report: Creating Loyalty That Lasts (2024). 143-page research report. Companion piece to The Value Gap and Winning New Clients (which focuses on prospects). This playbook focuses on existing clients across lifecycle.

The original language uses "financial advisers" and "wealth management" verbatim. For any other professional services firm, substitute the equivalent terms (see translation table in winning-new-clients-playbook.md).

The core thesis

Two critical insights about loyalty:

  1. Even as a client's perception of value grows, they may still leave. Average value rating rises across lifecycle (Newcomers 7.2 → Settled 7.8 → Loyals 8.2 out of 10), but clients still churn. The cost-as-proxy-for-not-enough-value reasoning + the "I can do this myself" overconfidence drive lapse.

  2. What clients value changes over time. Advisers must adapt their approach as the relationship matures. Static service offerings lose loyal clients.

Lifecycle segments

Segment Tenure Average value rating Overarching need
Newcomers < 3 years 7.2 / 10 Excited about the roadmap + empowered for autonomy
Settled 3-5 years 7.8 / 10 Recognise changing preferences + new tactics + knowledge to act on them
Loyals 6+ years 8.2 / 10 Acknowledge value + preferential service + reassurance of status quo

Why clients lapse

Among those who recently stopped using an adviser (top reasons): - 43% — expense or cost - 36% — wanting to manage finances themselves - 29% — "learnt all I could from that adviser" - 23% — finances not large/complex enough to have an adviser - 17% — not receiving value from adviser - 14% — not sure of quality - 10% — just stopped paying / lapsed

Critical insight: across all segments, self-rated financial literacy is dramatically higher than actual (Loyals: 43% claim high literacy, only 2% actually have it). The "I can do it myself" reason for lapsing is rooted in overconfidence bias.

The 12 value drivers

Each driver below has: behavioural-science backing, lifecycle-dependent emphasis, actions, and (where listed) named case-study exemplars (full entries in tenant-brand/gold-standard/entries/, tagged netwealth-sourced).

Driver 1 — Goal-based advice

Need: Goal-based planning reduces "temporal discounting" — humans undervalue distant benefits + overvalue immediate returns. When advisers provide a picture of how future goals can be met, those future rewards become more immediate, concrete, valuable. Creates "anticipatory utility" — pleasure from anticipating a future positive event.

Lifecycle emphasis: 74% of Loyals seek goal-based advice tailored to specific personal goals (vs. 60% Newcomers, 57% Settled). Personalisation deepens — Settled (50%) want culturally-informed advice; Loyals want flexible plans that adapt (64%) + co-creation where adviser "values and acts on my suggestions" (48%).

Actions: 1. Gamify the plan — short and long-term rewards, milestone engagement 2. Monitor life events — map financial milestones to personal goals 3. Celebrate early achievements — boost commitment and motivation 4. Address emotions — focus on emotional experience alongside financial goals 5. Scenario and what-if planning — visual simulations 6. Anticipate life goals/needs proactively — marriage, new home, birth of a child 7. Use long-term language: "We will be here to guide you through the ups and downs" / "Let's work together to create a plan that feels right for you." 8. Train staff on behavioural science — uncover loss aversion, overconfidence early. Use open-ended questions: "Can you provide me an example of a financial decision you're particularly pleased about — or one you regret?" / "If you were given $50,000 today, how would you use it?" 9. Co-create — involve clients in planning by offering options + educating on pros/cons 10. Grow cultural competency

Case studies: Lumiant · Duolingo · Second Nature

Driver 2 — Aligned investment options

Need: "Identity alignment" — people engage more with things that reflect their sense of identity. When clients see their ideals reflected in investments, they feel understood.

Lifecycle emphasis: 59% of Loyals say "recognising my investment preferences" is important (vs. 49% Newcomers). But "prioritises ethical or responsible options" peaks for Newcomers (51%) + Settled (57%) and drops for Loyals (43%). Newcomers slightly more want social/community-values investments (47% vs. 43%).

Actions: 1. Regularly discuss values — periodic reviews of evolving personal/community values 2. Leverage identity framing — "ethical investor" / "community-focused steward" 3. Regularly align portfolios to evolving preferences 4. Understand responsible investment options available — continually upgrade your knowledge 5. Provide transparency through reporting — show social/environmental impact 6. Minimise risk with what-if scenarios

Case studies: Aspiration · Conscious Investment Management · Aladdin Wealth (BlackRock)

Driver 3 — Guidance for the unexpected

Need: Reflects "loss aversion" — people are especially motivated to avoid negative outcomes. Reflects "authority bias" — clients trust professionals demonstrating deep specialised experience.

Lifecycle emphasis: Settled (72%) most value guidance on high-stakes decisions (home, business sale). Loyals (64%) expect specialised expertise on SMSF rules, tax regulations. Newcomers (51%) need support during major milestones.

Actions: 1. Use case studies — illustrate additional support during uncertain/complex decisions 2. Offer regular easy-to-understand updates — newsletter or podcast on regulatory and tax changes 3. Illustrate crisis support capabilities — clearly communicate expertise in death/divorce situations 4. Promote decision-making guidance for established clients 5. Showcase specialised knowledge — regulatory expertise, tax regulations

Case studies: Blueprint Wealth · Willful

Driver 4 — Innovative advice

Need: "Novelty effect" — fresh stimuli spark excitement, urgency, engagement. But interest fades as familiarity + trust deepen.

Lifecycle emphasis: Strong for Newcomers (43%) + Settled (50%), drops for Loyals (31%). Same pattern for "complex scenarios/structures" — 45% Newcomers, 48% Settled, 36% Loyals.

Actions: 1. Use language priming innovation: "Consider our advanced tax minimisation strategies" / "There are two new creative investment opportunities..." 2. Focus on early client touchpoints — emphasise innovation prominently in onboarding 3. Continually highlight the new — newsletters, meetings communicating added services 4. Offer specialised tools and resources — niche expansion, unique technologies

Case studies: Pivot Wealth

Driver 5 — Operational transparency

Need: Michael Norton's research on operational transparency — when people see the behind-the-scenes effort + rationale, they perceive the service as more valuable + trustworthy.

Lifecycle emphasis: Loyals continue to seek transparency (61% want fee clarity, vs. 55% Newcomers) — emphasising the need to avoid complacency. "Portfolio explanations" valued by all (~55%).

Actions: 1. Enhance the annual review — discuss fees, alternatives, service usage, value delivered 2. Deliver regular updates on service and value — detailed but easy-to-understand fee/service breakdown 3. Help people understand fees: a "fees FAQ" (perhaps via chatbot); comprehensive guide showing fee tiers + value delivered at each 4. Move to fixed-fee tiered arrangements — predictable pricing eliminates billing uncertainty; mirror professional services models from accounting/legal (Basic / Pro / Enterprise)

Case studies: Illuminate Wealth Management · Origin Energy · Two Trails Financial Planning

Driver 6 — Communicate often, proactively

Need: Addresses "uncertainty reduction" — humans naturally averse to uncertainty. Continuous information feed = greater sense of predictability + control.

Lifecycle emphasis: Importance INCREASES with tenure for almost all comms factors. Regular check-ins + portfolio reviews: 59% Loyals (vs. 43% Newcomers). Clear progress communication: 54% Loyals (vs. 36% Newcomers). Reliability during downturns: 43% Loyals (vs. 21% Newcomers — 22pp swing).

Actions: 1. Set clear 12-24 month expectations — potential market scenarios, anticipated returns 2. Communicate when things look bad — schedule consistent meetings on progress + concerns + adjustments 3. Regular portfolio performance reviews + market comparisons — clear, concise, timely updates 4. Consistent communication manages expectations — during downturns increase frequency for reassurance

Case studies: Morgan Stanley Next Best Action (Netwealth's own portfolio reporting tech also cited as an example, but not extracted as separate entry — it's the operator's own product)

Driver 7 — Continual reassurance beyond markets

Need: "Confirmation bias" — people seek out information that confirms existing beliefs/decisions. Continual reassurance about adviser expertise helps clients feel validated.

Lifecycle emphasis: 59% of Loyals want continual assurance of adviser's track record (vs. 47% Newcomers). 51% Newcomers value ties to other financial specialists (vs. 32% — actually higher for Newcomers). Loyals appreciate broader partnerships (46%).

Actions: 1. Share annual highlights of team's education + accreditations 2. Your networks are their networks — share case studies and success stories from other clients 3. Create case studies as social proof — highlight professional networks 4. Establish peer networks and groups — regular meetings/workshops/forums

Case studies: Entrepreneurs' Organization

Driver 8 — Knowledge for empowerment

Need: "Self-determination theory" — people most motivated when they feel capable (competence) + in control of choices (autonomy).

Lifecycle emphasis: Newcomers (62%) want to be empowered with autonomy tools — drops to 46% for Loyals. Newcomers (62%) want adviser to "tell them something they don't know" — drops to 36% for Loyals. But Loyals want help "explaining complex concepts" (59% vs. 36% Newcomers — flips entirely).

Actions: 1. Offer targeted financial education — tailored to segment (Newcomers: foundations; Settled: niche strategies; Loyals: investment vehicles like MDAs) 2. Multi-format information: educational workshops, online learning centre, live Q&A sessions

Case studies: Ellevest · Flux

Driver 9 — Prompt and timely support

Need: Reciprocity — feeling like a priority reduces friction that pushes clients elsewhere.

Lifecycle emphasis: Speed expectations INCREASE with tenure. 51% Loyals + 50% Settled want instant support / same-day responses (vs. 43% Newcomers). 56% Loyals + 52% Settled want ease of getting in touch (vs. 43% Newcomers).

Actions: 1. Set internal service standards — KPIs, processes, exception reporting 2. Communicate service standards to set expectations — anchoring principle 3. Over-communicate reliability — mention in newsletters how regularly you meet standards 4. Offer multiple communication channels — phone, email, online chat, video 5. Use technology — CRM, Knowledge Centre, helpdesk software 6. Introduce online self-service tools — secure portal, chatbots, scheduling

Case studies: Ryanair · Bessemer Trust · Capital One Eno · Morgan Stanley AskResearchGPT

Driver 10 — Frictionless digital experience

Need: "Cognitive ease" — people prefer formats easy to access, process, manage. "Social presence theory" — face-to-face for complex matters.

Lifecycle emphasis: Newcomers crave digitisation, becomes less important as relationship matures: - 55% Newcomers value online portal info availability vs. 38% Loyals - 47% Newcomers want self-service features vs. 33% Loyals - 45% Newcomers want real-time portfolio updates vs. 34% Loyals

But BOTH Settled (64%) + Loyals (48%) value streamlined processes/paperwork (vs. 36% Newcomers) — they appreciate back-end tech making things smoother without extra effort.

Actions: - Understand the importance of a client portal + mobile app as the cornerstone of digital-first experience - 48% of "AdviceTech Stars" use a client portal today, 43% intend to adopt in next 24 months - 79% of client-portal-using firms gain client engagement benefit; 68% gain communications benefit

Case studies: (Netwealth's own client portal cited; not extracted as separate entry)

Driver 11 — Rewarding clients

Need: "Reciprocity" — when people receive a benefit, they feel compelled to return the favour. "Scarcity" — limited availability increases perceived value.

Lifecycle emphasis: Loyalty incentives expected by all (53% Newcomers, 50% Settled, 56% Loyals). Exclusive client benefits + dedicated wealth manager scale with tenure (48% Newcomers → 56% Loyals). Referral rewards peak for Newcomers (51% vs. 36% Loyals).

Most-valued additional services (2022 Advisable Australian): - Fee rebates (64%) - Market trends/insights/research (60%) - Special product offers (45%) - Priority service / call access (44%) - Travel and lifestyle perks (42%) - Networking opportunities (37%) - Reward point schemes (37%) - Thought leadership content (37%) - Exclusive events (33%)

Actions: 1. Waive certain fees for Loyals — selective or rebates 2. Provide valuable resources as benefit — white papers, webinars, industry reports 3. Offer a loyalty programme — points-based 4. Personalised gifts 5. Priority access to advisers — tenure-based booking priority 6. Anniversary celebrations — handwritten thank-you cards, milestones at 1/5/10 years

Case studies: Bank of America Preferred Rewards (Netwealth's own Investor Rewards Programme also cited, not extracted)

Driver 12 — Contributing to the world

Need: "Social proof" + identity alignment with broader societal values (environment, community).

Lifecycle emphasis: 47% of Newcomers value environmental impact (vs. 25% Loyals — 22pp drop). But 44% of Loyals expect community impact (vs. 36% Newcomers).

Actions:

Environment: 1. Responsible investment options + impact reports 2. Impact investing networks for like-minded clients 3. Impact storytelling and reporting via case studies (video, photo, on-the-ground stories) 4. Sustainable business practices (carbon emissions, renewables)

Community: 5. Support social causes through donations/partnerships/volunteering 6. Employee volunteer programmes — paid time off, matched donations 7. Pro bono / discounted services — free financial literacy workshops 8. Sponsor local events 9. Support community impact funds

Both: 10. Publish annual sustainability + community impact reports

Lifecycle segment profiles (cross-driver summary)

Newcomers (<3 years) — "Set me up for success"

Show me the possibilities: - Prioritises ethical or responsible investments - Creative financial and investment strategies beyond conventions - Helps navigate complex scenarios (foreign investments, tax structures, estate planning) - Connects me to other financial services (e.g. accountant) - Provides guidance for crucial decisions

Empower me for autonomy: - Empowering me with confidence + tools for autonomous decisions - Availability of information via online portals/apps - Self-service options

Other: - Transparency in fees and services - Incentives, benefits, discounts for referrals - Positive role impacting the environment

Settled (3-5 years) — "Build my wealth muscle"

Understand me better: - Culturally informed financial advice - Recognises my preferences in investments - Helps navigate complex scenarios/structures

Challenge and continue to educate me: - Creative + innovative advice strategies - Has connections with other financial partners/platforms - Empowers confidence + autonomy - Well-designed financial information

Be there for me: - Instant support (same-day responses) - Ease of getting in touch - Reliability during downturns - Online portal/app access - Self-service options

Reward my loyalty: - Referral incentives - Exclusive client benefits (dedicated wealth manager)

Be involved in my world: - Positive community role

Loyals (6+ years) — "Recognise me and be there for me"

Recognise my abilities: - Values and acts on my suggestions - Recognises my preferences - Advice tailored to specific personal goals - Helps navigate regulatory changes (SMSF, tax)

Remind me why we should be partnering / Don't get apathetic: - Transparency in fees and services - Connections with other financial partners - Track record, consistency, tangible outcomes - Relevant education + professional accreditations

Reward my loyalty: - Exclusive client benefits (dedicated wealth manager)

Be there for me: - Reliability during downturns - Clearly communicates progress against strategy - Regular check-ins + portfolio reviews - Instant support - Ease of getting in touch - Streamlined processes + paperwork

Challenge and continue to educate me: - Transparency in fees and services - Referral incentives - Positive role impacting environment

Be involved in my world: - Positive community role

All segments

Build and evolve my plan: - Flexible plans personalised to financial goals - Goal-focused financial planning - Sets early 12-24 month expectations - Regular updates against agreed goals - Expanding financial horizons

Maximise my returns: - Returns within risk tolerance - Investment opportunities aligned with social/community values

Help me during uncertainty: - Help navigating unexpected life events - Transparency on poor-performing investments + optional solutions

Give me peace of mind: - Assurance investments perform in line with market - Security and comfort professional management - Relevant education + professional accreditations

Reward my loyalty: - Loyalty incentives (reduced fees for longstanding clients)

How to apply to non-wealth-management professional services

Same translation table as in winning-new-clients-playbook.md applies. The 12 drivers translate cleanly: - Legal: "matter outcomes" instead of "investment performance"; "named partner" instead of "dedicated wealth manager"; "tiered legal-services packages" instead of fixed-fee investment packages - Accounting: "engagement scope" instead of "portfolio"; "advisory services" instead of "investment strategies" - Consulting: "engagement deliverables" instead of "portfolio performance"; "tiered consulting packages" - Agency: "campaign performance" instead of "portfolio performance"; "retainer tiers" - Coaching: "programme outcomes" instead of "investment returns"; "programme tiers"

The lifecycle stages (Newcomers / Settled / Loyals) translate directly to any retainer-based or repeat-engagement professional services business. The behavioural-science principles are industry-agnostic.
