Winning New Clients — The Value Gap

Authored by the operator, originally published as Netwealth IQ Report: The Value Gap and Winning New Clients (2024). Research conducted with The Lab Strategy — qualitative (14 in-depth interviews) + quantitative (450 Australian respondents). Behavioural-science framework: COM-B (Michie, van Stralen & West, 2011) + temporal-discounting (Hershfield, 2011).

The original language uses "financial advisers" and "wealth management" verbatim. For any other professional services firm (legal, accounting, consulting, agency, coaching), substitute the equivalent terms — the strategic spine is identical.

The core thesis

Many people recognise the benefits of engaging a professional services firm, yet fail to commit the time and money to one. Like fitness intentions — the intention is present, but the action is missing. In wealth management, prospects are stuck as "considerers" — and the gap between intention and action is the value gap.

The value gap isn't usually about price alone. It's about deep-rooted emotional drivers — loss aversion, present-bias, overconfidence — that perception-change can address.

The five barriers to advice

Barrier What it is Behavioural science
1. Loss aversion The most significant reported barrier — 51% of prospects cite perceived cost of advice. Prospects view the cost as a loss from current position, overshadowing potential gains. Loss aversion
2. Present focus 30% of prospects say "other things in my life distract me and I put the decision off." 27% indicate they "haven't been able to find advice yet." Present-biased preferences (Hershfield)
3. "I'm not right for this" 25% of prospects — especially Emerging Affluent + Established Mass — believe their financial situations are too simple. "My finances aren't big enough." Misconception that advice is only for the wealthy. Self-categorisation bias
4. Overconfidence 59% of prospects self-report moderate financial literacy, 23% claim high — but objective assessment shows only 15% actually have high literacy. The younger Emerging Affluent overestimate the most (47% claim high, 22% actual). Overconfidence bias / Dunning-Kruger
5. Cost / willingness-to-pay disconnect When shown "typical" advice packages at market rate ($5,500 / $7,500 / $12,500 p.a.), less than half of suitability-confirmed prospects had any willingness to pay at those price points. Reference anchors are 30-50% below market. Anchoring (Kahneman/Tversky)

Implication: marketing isn't just about communicating service value — it's about reshaping the perception anchors prospects bring to the decision.

The Netwealth Value-for-Money Model

Three dimensions of value, informed by COM-B framework. Each dimension has 14 thematic drivers underneath.

Three dimensions

Dimension What it means % of prospects ranking as most important
Motivation — "helps me achieve my goals" Aligning services with the personal ambitions and desires of the individual 53% (primary driver)
Ease — "makes it easy to find and access value" Simplifying interactions so the process feels straightforward and user-friendly 34%
Context — "has contextual associations and heuristics for value" Cultural alignment, social endorsements, professional brand bolstering confidence 13%

Three keys for influencing perception

Key Explanation
What — The Value-for-Money Model The 14 drivers below define what to focus on
How — Language Clarity, framing, social norms, motivation/self-efficacy, emotional resonance
When — Touchpoints on path-to-purchase Referral → website/social → meeting(s) → if not converted, ongoing nurture (newsletters, podcasts, webinars)

Motivation drivers (6) — show prospects you understand their goals

Driver M1 — Trust and transparency (74%)

Need: Prospects need to feel confident in the integrity of the firm. Reflects reciprocity principle. Established Mass especially (76%).

Language to use: - "Our advisers act with the highest integrity and honesty." - "Transparent fees and services, with no hidden costs." - "Independence and neutrality in our advice ensures your best interests are always at heart."

Tactics: 1. Showcase integrity: highlight advisers' credentials + ethical standards prominently. 2. Transparent communication: detailed, easy-to-understand fee + service breakdowns including how you invest. 3. Build trust with case studies: demonstrate transparency + success. 4. Client testimonials: existing clients share positive experiences. 5. Personal introductions: "meet the team" opportunity or introductory video.

A/B test result: Fee communication preference — 77% preferred Concept 1B ("$700/month. This fee pays for: [4 specific things]") over Concept 1A ("$700/month to help you make and execute your financial plan").

Driver M2 — Personalised quality advice (63%)

Need: Tailored strategies + feeling seen and understood. Higher for Established Affluent (64%) and Established Mass (67%).

Language: - "Tailored advice that meets your unique financial goals and needs." - "Flexible plans designed to fit your specific financial situation." - "We value your input and incorporate your suggestions into our strategies."

Tactics: Client empathy (profiling tools), co-creation (involve client in planning), recalibration reviews, personalised roadmaps, proactive monitoring, interactive reporting tools, highlight personal success (case studies of similar profiles).

A/B test results: - 64% preferred Concept 2B (specific average upside of $70,000/year) over 2A (general value claim) - 70% preferred Concept 3B (emotional/goal-focused outcomes — "shape your financial future around the important goals you want to achieve") over 3A (investment-return-focused)

Implication: prospects respond better to emotional, goal-focused outcomes than to investment-return language — especially early in the relationship.

Driver M3 — Creative & innovative advice (54%)

Need: Creative financial strategies that go beyond conventional norms. Higher for Established Affluent (64%) and Established Mass (67%).

Language: - "Innovative strategies for optimised financial growth." - "Cutting-edge tools and analytics to manage your portfolio." - "Advanced tax minimisation strategies and debt management advice."

Tactics: Highlight unique opportunities, educational workshops, demonstrate tools/dashboards, showcase success of innovative strategies.

Driver M4 — Immediacy / responsive engagement (52%)

Need: Real-time updates, quick feedback, regular check-ins, rapid portfolio adjustments.

Language: - "Stay informed with real-time updates and quick responses." - "Regular check-ins and portfolio reviews to keep you on track." - "Rapid adjustments to market conditions to protect your investments."

Tactics: Portfolio tracking + reporting tools, highlight timely interventions with case studies, proactive communication, quick feedback (same-day responses).

Driver M5 — Positive emotions / free and happy (46%)

Need: Positive, fulfilling experience contributing to overall sense of financial wellbeing + happiness.

Language: - "Experience the joy of financial freedom and security." - "We make you feel valued and not just a number." - "A delightful and fulfilling financial planning experience."

Tactics: Positive atmosphere in all initial interactions, celebrate achievements (case studies of milestones), exclusive events, case studies focused on emotional experience rather than financial outcomes.

Driver M6 — Client rewards (32%)

Need: Acknowledgement of loyalty, reciprocity. Emerging Affluent place higher importance (37%).

Language: - "Enjoy exclusive benefits and rewards for your loyalty." - "Incentives for referrals and long-standing clients." - "Exclusive access to dedicated wealth managers and networking opportunities."

Tactics: Highlight unique benefits, loyalty programme (reduced fees / exclusive product offers), dedicated wealth manager criteria, networking opportunities, referral incentives.

Ease drivers (5) — make it easy to find and access value

Driver E1 — Long-term strategic guidance (71%)

Need: Knowing how long-term goals of growth and preservation will be supported, especially in unexpected events (buying a house, selling a business, divorce).

Language: - "We focus on long-term wealth growth and preservation." - "Financial planning to meet your future aspirations." - "Guidance through crucial financial decisions and life events." - "Our streamlined process ensures you understand every step of your financial journey. Here's how we make financial planning easy for you."

Tactics: Guidance on major decisions, step-by-step guides, success stories of clients navigating significant milestones.

A/B test result: 57% preferred Concept 4B ("simple chat → easy steps → smooth, supportive process — financial advice, simplified") over 4A ("initial consultation → detailed meetings → personalised plan → regular check-ins"). Ease and simplicity language outperforms comprehensiveness language.

Driver E2 — Security, support and reassurance (62%)

Need: Regular updates against goals, assurance of market-aligned performance, professional management. Established Affluent especially (67%).

Language: - "We provide regular updates on your progress and our strategic plans." - "We're here for you at every step. Expect regular updates and personalised reports to keep you informed." - "We work to ensure that your investments align with market conditions." - "You can have comfort in knowing your wealth is managed professionally."

Tactics: Proactive communication system, market alignment assurances, set early 12-24 month expectations, track record + case studies of secure-feeling clients.

Driver E3 — Knowledge for empowerment (59%)

Need: Not just advice but learning + understanding. Prospects want to challenge themselves with new perspectives. Empowers autonomous decision-making.

Language: - "We empower you with the knowledge to make informed decisions." - "We provide clear explanations of complex financial concepts." - "Access our range of educational events and materials to enhance your financial literacy." - "Empower yourself with our educational resources. Join our next webinar to learn how to manage your finances effectively."

Tactics: Educational workshops, interactive tools/calculators, personalised learning paths, multi-format education (blog + podcast + interactive sessions).

Driver E4 — Emotional support (34%)

Need: Reducing mental load, lowering anxiety, addressing unspoken emotional needs. Established Mass especially (36%).

Language: - "We help reduce your mental load and financial anxiety." - "We provide support to manage your emotional responses to finances." - "We coach you through tough financial decisions." - "We help you make rational financial decisions."

Tactics: Emotional coaching positioning, client stories of emotional support, networking events (client appreciation evenings, exclusive workshops).

Driver E5 — Seamless accessibility (32%)

Need: Frictionless engagement — self-service via apps/portals, easy admin, easy onboarding. Established Affluent especially.

Language: - "Quick and responsive communication to meet your needs." - "Easily access your financial information and manage your portfolio online." - "Streamlined processes for a hassle-free experience." - "We take care of the paperwork." - "Contact us by the mode that best works for you — email, phone or other."

Tactics: Self-service options (mobile app, client portal), streamlined onboarding + paperwork, multi-mode communication.

Context drivers (3) — provide contextual cues for confidence

Driver C1 — Community endorsement (46%)

Need: Endorsements as decision signal. Reflects conformity principle. Established Mass especially (55%).

Language: - "Our advisers are certified by the top financial institutions and adhere to the highest standards of professionalism." - "Join the hundreds of satisfied clients who have trusted us with their financial futures." - "Hear directly from clients like you who have achieved their financial goals with our guidance." - "See why thousands trust us with their financial future — join our next webinar featuring experts from [...]." - "Rated #1 by [credible source] for customer satisfaction in financial advising." - "Recommended by..." - "Join us this month as we discuss key financial strategies in our community forum featuring success stories from clients just like you."

Tactics: Highlight education + accreditations, dynamic social norms (regularly communicate how many clients benefit), client success stories (video testimonial series featuring diverse backgrounds), highlight associations with renowned brands, partner visibility, build community (forums / groups / webinars / local events), professional brand image consistent across touchpoints.

Driver C2 — Social associations / endorsement (33%)

Need: Associations with industry bodies, renowned brands, other financial partners. Emerging Affluent place higher importance on visibility + image (41%).

Combined with C1 tactically — same content surfaces serve both.

Driver C3 — Cultural resonance (30%)

Need: Advice that respects + aligns with cultural values, team diversity, ethical/responsible investing.

Language: - "We provide financial advice that respects and aligns with your cultural values." - "Our team's diverse backgrounds ensure we understand and cater to your unique needs." - "We are committed to ethical and responsible investments." - "Our commitment to community and environmental impact sets us apart."

Tactics: Culturally informed advice positioning, highlight team diversity / personal stories / cultural expertise, showcase firm's community involvement, align investment opportunities with social/community values.

Cross-cutting principles

Path-to-purchase touchpoint sequencing

Typical prospect journey: 1. Referral (or initial brand discovery) 2. Website / social (research phase — digital surfaces do the heavy lifting) 3. Multiple meetings (consideration phase) 4. Ongoing nurture (if not converted) — newsletters, podcasts, webinars, content marketing

The Motivation / Ease / Context model applies AT EVERY TOUCHPOINT. The driver weight shifts: Context-signals matter most at discovery (1-2), Ease + Motivation matter most through consideration (3), Motivation drives commitment (3→4).

Language as behavioural lever

Five mechanisms: 1. Clarity — unambiguous information enables informed decisions 2. Framing — gain-frames vs. loss-frames change behaviour 3. Social norm shaping — "most people…" encourages compliance 4. Self-efficacy — "you can do it" boosts confidence 5. Emotional appeals — emotional content prompts action

Behavioural-science principles invoked (full list)

Loss aversion · Present-biased preferences · Overconfidence bias · Anchoring · Reciprocity · Authority bias · Conformity · Social proof · Availability heuristic · Cognitive ease · Familiarity · Habit formation.
