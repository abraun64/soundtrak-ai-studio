# 📷 Editorial documentary

The aesthetic of *The New York Times Magazine* + *National Geographic* + *Magnum Photos* — applied to brand work. Real people photographed in real settings, with editorial-photographer-level craft. The audience reads this register as "this is true, not sold to me."

## When to pick this for §4 Visual architecture

- Brand needs to feel credible / trustworthy / serious
- Audience is sophisticated and allergic to glossy advertising
- Identity reinforcement work where the protagonist must be a real customer/user, not a model
- B2B credibility work where over-produced visuals would signal "vendor trying too hard"
- Cause-led or social-stance campaigns where authenticity is the point

## Visual hallmarks

- Natural light, often available-light, often imperfect
- Real environments (kitchens, factories, streets, workplaces) — not seamless backdrops
- Casting: real subjects, often non-actors; visible age, weight, texture, weariness
- Composition: photojournalistic — subject often off-centre, environmental context visible
- Colour: muted, slightly desaturated; or warm document-film palette (think Kodak Portra)
- Wardrobe: subject's own clothes, not styled
- Typography pairing: serif (humanist or transitional) more often than sans; restrained sizing
- Motion equivalent: handheld camera, long-take observation, ambient sound, minimal cuts

## What a CD writing §4 Visual architecture for this register would say

> *Mood: quiet, observational, unstaged — the texture of a real day, not the highlight reel.
> Palette: muted desaturated tones, warm shadows, Kodak Portra register; no high-saturation accents.
> Typography: humanist serif (Lyon, Tiempos), restrained hierarchy; treat headlines like editorial decks not display advertising.
> Imagery: editorial documentary photography, single subjects, environmental context, natural light. Real users, real wardrobe, no models. Photographed by an editorial photographer with a real photojournalism credit, not a brand photographer.
> Explicit no-go: stock photography, seamless white backdrops, over-saturated colour grading, models with perfect teeth, motion-graphics overlays.*

## Anti-patterns

- **Documentary-veneer**: real-looking film with scripted-feeling moments. Audiences spot the seam in 5 seconds.
- **Casting tokenism**: "diverse real people" cast for diversity quota without specificity to story — feels worse than no diversity attempt
- **Over-grading**: documentary photography that's been colour-graded into commercial polish — undoes the register
- **Set-dressed "real homes"**: when the "real" environment is obviously styled, the campaign reverts to lifestyle ad
- **Documentary-as-template**: every brand chasing this register has crowded the position; freshness requires craft elevation beyond the average
