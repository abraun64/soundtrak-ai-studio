# 🎨 Maximalist / chaotic

The aesthetic of *Liquid Death* + *Old Spice* + *Duolingo TikTok* + *MSCHF* + Memphis-revival design + early-2000s skate culture + tabloid layout — fast, saturated, unapologetic, deliberately abrasive against category convention. Audiences read this register as "this brand has a sense of humour and respects mine."

## When to pick this for §4 Visual architecture

- Participatory campaigns where the audience needs visual ammo to share
- Challenger-brand work that needs to break a sleepy category's visual sameness
- Categories the audience finds boring (water, insurance, paperwork SaaS, B2B) where irreverent visuals = memorability arbitrage
- Younger audiences (Gen Z especially) that read polish as untrustworthy and rough-edge as authentic
- Wild-posture concepts pushing brand stretch hard

## Visual hallmarks

- High-saturation colour: neon, hot pink, electric blue, acid green — colours that aggressively *don't* go with each other
- Type: oversized display fonts, mixed weights and styles in one composition, deliberate clash, often using free/system fonts as an aesthetic statement
- Composition: layered, stacked, busy; rejection of negative space; meme-grammar (impact font + image, screenshots, "POV:" framing)
- Imagery: high-contrast photography, deliberate roughness, blown-out flash, distorted/cropped weirdly, often appropriated stock-as-anti-stock
- Motion: fast cuts, zoom-punches, text overlays, TikTok-native vertical
- Packaging-as-medium: when a CPG brand uses this register, the packaging itself is the loudest asset (Liquid Death is the canonical case)
- Tone in copy: profanity, slang, deliberate misspellings, ALL CAPS, capitalisation chaos

## What a CD writing §4 Visual architecture for this register would say

> *Mood: loud, irreverent, anti-category-convention — a deliberate punch in the face of every quiet competitor.
> Palette: high-saturation; pick 2 colours that aggressively don't go together and lean in. Neon yellow + ultraviolet, hot pink + acid green, blood red + electric blue.
> Typography: oversized display fonts, mixed weights and styles in one composition, deliberate hierarchy chaos. Free/system fonts welcome as an aesthetic statement.
> Imagery: high-contrast, blown-out flash, deliberate rough crops, meme-grammar (POV framing, text overlays, screenshot aesthetic). Appropriated stock used ironically.
> Motion: TikTok-native vertical, fast cuts, zoom-punches, text overlays riding the visuals.
> Packaging (if relevant): the loudest asset. Designed to be screenshot at retail and posted.
> Tone in copy: lean toward profanity, slang, ALL CAPS, deliberate misspellings — taste-test against brand permission.
> Explicit no-go: muted palette, restrained typography, white space, anything that reads as "tasteful" by trade-press standards.*

## Anti-patterns

- **Cargo-cult chaos**: brand without permission to be loud copying the surface — feels like dad trying to be hip
- **Maximalism without strategy**: visual chaos that's just chaos, not in service of an idea — exhausting and forgettable
- **Tone-incongruent brand fit**: regulated, family-positioned, or B2B-conservative brands attempting this register — instantly cringe
- **Single-execution chaos**: works once as a stunt; the brand can't sustain the cadence; brand drifts back to muted, the chaos becomes a relic
- **Polish-creep**: maximalist work that's been agency-polished into commercial gloss — the rough edge IS the appeal; sanding it off kills it
- **Audience mismatch**: chaos register reads as "trying too hard" to audiences older than ~35 unless paired with cultural literacy (Liquid Death threads this needle; many imitators don't)
