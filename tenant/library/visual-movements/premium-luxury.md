# 💎 Premium / luxury

The register of *Aesop*, *Rolex*, *Aman*, *Bottega Veneta*, *Loro Piana*, and considered high-end spirits. Not "expensive-looking" via gold and gloss, but richness through **restraint, material honesty, and craft**. Space, stillness, and quality-of-detail signal value; the audience reads "this brand doesn't need to shout."

## When to pick this for §4 Visual architecture

- Luxury / premium / high-ticket considered purchases where price is justified by *feeling*, not spec
- Brands whose value is craft, heritage, materials, or exclusivity
- Identity reinforcement for aspirational audiences ("this is who I am")
- Categories where mass-market cues (bright, busy, discounted) would cheapen the brand

## Visual hallmarks

- Generous space + stillness; nothing rushed, nothing crowded — "expensive emptiness"
- Palette: muted, tonal — warm neutrals, deep jewel tones, or monochrome; gold used sparingly if at all
- Typography: refined high-contrast serif or elegant display; deliberate, tight spacing; never loud
- Imagery: exceptional-craft photography — texture, material, light; close, tactile, editorial
- Composition: art-directed, asymmetric-but-balanced
- Motion: slow, cinematic, unhurried
- Copy register: sparse, evocative, confident; understatement as power

## What a CD writing §4 for this register would say

> Mood: rich through restraint — space, material, and craft carry the value; nothing shouts. Palette: muted and tonal (warm neutrals or deep jewel tones), gold only as a whisper. Typography: a refined high-contrast serif or elegant display, deliberate spacing. Imagery: tactile, exceptional-craft photography — texture and light, editorial framing. Composition: art-directed asymmetry with generous emptiness. Motion: slow, cinematic. Copy: sparse, evocative; understatement. No-go: gold-and-gloss clichés, busy layouts, discount cues, stock photography, bright saturated colour.

## Anti-patterns

- **Bling-as-luxury**: gold + gloss + serifs applied as a skin — reads as *trying* to look expensive, which reads as not.
- **Cold sterility**: restraint without warmth or craft — empty, not premium.
- **Mass-market tells**: one "SALE" or busy promo module snaps the whole illusion.
- **Craft-claim without craft**: premium cues on a product that isn't — the gap shows fast.
