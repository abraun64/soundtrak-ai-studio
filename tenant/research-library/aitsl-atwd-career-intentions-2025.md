# Australian Teacher Workforce Data — Wellbeing & Career Intentions

**Publisher**: AITSL (Australian Institute for Teaching and School Leadership), Australian Teacher Workforce Data (ATWD) programme.
**Dates**: *In Focus: Teacher Wellbeing & Career Intentions*, **October 2025 edition**; underlying **2023 Australian Teacher Workforce Survey** (n = 50,556 teachers; 22,509 completed the wellbeing module).
**Added by**: Phase-1 insight scan (ACME CO Global Education Workforce Report), 2026-06-22.
**Segment relevance**: teacher candidate pool (supply) — primary; school decision-makers (AU) — secondary.

## Citation / links

## Faithful summary — key data points
- **Career intentions (2023):** 26% intend to stay until retirement · 35% unsure · **39% intend to leave before retirement** (5% within 1 yr · 9% within 2–4 yrs · 10% within 5–9 yrs · 6% in 10+ yrs · 8% unsure when).
- **Top reasons for intending to leave:** heavy workload **75%** · better work-life balance **69%** · stress / mental-health impact **68%** · administrative workload **63%** · insufficient pay **52%** · professional-regulation demands **50%**. (Pay ranks *fifth* — the driver is workload/conditions, not headline salary.)
- Teachers **aged 30–49** most likely to intend to leave before retirement. **Casual/relief teachers (CRTs)** and senior leaders most likely to intend to stay to retirement (skews older); CRTs under 60 more often cite **job security** as a stressor.
- Workload, work-life balance and work-related stress have been the **top three reasons consistently since 2019**.
- State signal (corroborating): WA recorded 1,279 teacher resignations in 2024–25, a five-year high and **+120% since 2020**.
- Important caveat (AITSL's own): reported intent-to-leave of 30–40% (yes/no format) tends to translate to **actual turnover closer to 10–15% over 5 years** — intent overstates exits. Use intent as a *sentiment/dissatisfaction* signal, not a forecast.

## Why it matters for ACME CO
Evidences the supply-side tension precisely: the educators ACME CO depends on are dissatisfied with **conditions and respect**, not primarily pay — which validates ACME CO's "treat teachers as professionals" supply-side promise as on-tension, and warns *against* a pay-only candidate hook. The CRT job-security stressor + the WA resignation spike feed the candidate-facing pay/benchmark utility framing.
