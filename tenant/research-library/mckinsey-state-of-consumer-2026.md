## Faithful summary — key findings
- **Selective spending, not a broad pullback.** Consumers spend *selectively* rather than cutting discretionary across the board — "spending smarter, not just more." US sentiment has softened, with tariffs now the #2 worry behind inflation; younger cohorts adjust behaviour faster than boomers.
- **Splurging on experiences.** The pull toward meaningful, memorable, restorative moments outweighs the instinct to cut back — the global experiences market grew ~2.6% (2023–2025), near its pre-COVID rate.
- **"Online and alone" is permanent.** Digital-first, at-home behaviours from the pandemic have stuck: >90% of US and Chinese consumers shopped an online-only retailer in the past month (>80% in Germany / UK).
- **Wellness, broadly defined.** Health now spans heart, cognitive function, sleep, stress, and gut — most consumers say these matter, yet fewer than half feel they're achieving their wellness goals (a durable unmet-need gap).
- **GenAI reshapes discovery — brand sites barely feature.** Only ~1% of the sources LLMs cite in consumer-goods answers come from brand-owned websites; a brand's own site plays a minimal role in how GenAI represents it, shifting discovery power toward third-party / earned content.
- **Generational split.** Gen Z shows distinct patterns (value-seeking + experience-led + "online and alone"); behaviour change is concentrated in younger cohorts.

## Why it matters (for any consumer-facing tenant)
The through-line is a **value-conscious consumer who still pays up for meaning** — so the winning message isn't "cheaper," it's "worth it": justify price with experience, health outcome, or identity, not discounts. Two cross-vertical implications for marketing: (1) the **GenAI-discovery finding** (~1% brand-site citation) says owned media + earned / third-party presence now govern how AI describes a brand — a direct argument for the owned-media / authority thesis, relevant even to B2B; (2) the **wellness gap** (matters to most, achieved by few) is a durable unmet-need to position against. Portable to any B2C tenant; the discovery finding is portable to all.
