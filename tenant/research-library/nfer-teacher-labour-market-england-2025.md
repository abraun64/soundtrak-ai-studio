## Faithful summary — key data points
- **Unfilled teacher vacancy rate ~6 per 1,000 teachers in 2023/24 — double the pre-pandemic rate and ~6x the 2010/11 level.**
- Recruitment into initial teacher training (ITT) remains persistently below target; leaving rates have not meaningfully improved since the pandemic.
- **Subject concentration:** physics ITT has filled **under 30% of funded places** in recent years (shortfall **>70% of target in 2024/25** per Tes). **45% of school leaders** report high levels of non-specialists teaching some maths lessons.
- Secondary schools — especially those with more deprived intakes — increasingly rely on unqualified and non-specialist teachers.
- Real-terms context: the 5.5% pay rise + £30k starting salaries returned starting pay only to ~2010/11 real-terms levels; the Early Career Framework has had little measured retention impact.

## Why it matters for ACME CO
The shortage is **not uniform — it's concentrated in specialist subjects, SEND and deprived/regional schools** ("hard-to-fill" = ACME CO's open-territory claim per playbook §0). This evidences the school-DM tension (the *hardest* roles are precisely the ones the generic market can't fill) and the specialist-free-agent candidate scarcity. Supports a report cut that names *where* the shortage actually bites rather than the generic "teacher shortage" headline everyone already owns.
