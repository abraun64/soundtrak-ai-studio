## Faithful summary — key data points
- **Nearly half of school principals in OECD countries report teacher shortages hinder instruction — up sharply from 29% in 2015.** (A leadership-felt, not abstract, metric.)
- Pipeline of teachers **under 30 declines with level taught**: 17% pre-primary → 13% primary → **just 9% at secondary** — the secondary specialist pipeline is structurally thin.
- Ageing workforce + declining birth rates + regional distribution disparities compound the shortage; future retirements threaten sustainability without stronger recruitment in hard-to-staff subjects/regions.

## Why it matters for ACME CO
The "**shortages hinder instruction, up from 29% to ~50%**" figure converts the abstract shortage into a **leadership decision-maker's lived problem** — useful for the school-DM and buyer cuts. The under-30-pipeline-at-9% figure underpins the *specialist scarcity* story (pairs with NFER physics + AITSL). Macro/authoritative anchor; not a standalone campaign insight.
