# Self-Determination Theory at Work — what professionals actually want

**Vertical**: universal · **Layer**: human-behaviour · **Audience**: all (professionals / knowledge workers) · **Topic**: motivation / what people want from work
**Added by**: Phase-1 insight scan — Run 2 (human layer), ACME CO Global Education Workforce Report, 2026-06-22.
**Segment relevance**: the values backbone under the teacher (3.1) and relief-pool (3.2) human insights; broadly applicable to any worker/candidate audience.

## Sources / citation
- **Deci, E. L. & Ryan, R. M.** — Self-Determination Theory (SDT): the three basic psychological needs of **autonomy · competence · relatedness**.
- **Pink, D.**, *Drive* (2009) — the popular synthesis: **autonomy · mastery · purpose** as the conditions for high performance in creative/knowledge work.
- *Understanding and shaping the future of work with self-determination theory*, **PMC review** — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9088153/
- Gallup recognition research — employees who receive regular recognition are more engaged and more likely to stay.

## Faithful summary — key findings
- Three needs must be met to motivate sustainably: **autonomy** (choosing how you work, not having methods dictated), **competence / mastery** (feeling effective; opportunities to extend your capability), and **relatedness** (meaningful connection / belonging).
- Pink's restatement adds **purpose** (being part of something bigger and impactful) as a potent driver for meaningful work.
- Meta-analytic evidence: satisfying these needs is associated with **better performance, reduced burnout, more organizational commitment and reduced turnover intentions**. Their *absence* drives disengagement and exit.
- Recognition / being valued is a distinct, well-evidenced retention lever (Gallup).

## Why it matters (for any tenant; first used for ACME CO)
The "why" beneath dissatisfaction surveys: people don't leave (only) over hours or money — they leave when autonomy, mastery, belonging and purpose are denied. For ACME CO it explains *why* the teacher exit is a vocation/respect wound (autonomy + relatedness denied) and why the relief pool's pain is a status/dignity wound (relatedness + competence denied — being treated as interchangeable cover). Universal: the canonical evidence base for "what professionals want from work," reusable for any candidate-, employee-, or member-facing tenant.
