# Teacher Moral Injury & the Wounded Vocation — why teachers really leave

**Vertical**: universal · **Layer**: human-behaviour · **Audience**: educators (candidate pool) · **Topic**: vocation / professional identity / why teachers leave
**Added by**: Phase-1 insight scan — Run 2 (human layer), ACME CO Global Education Workforce Report, 2026-06-22.
**Segment relevance**: teacher candidate pool (supply) — primary; the deep human layer beneath AITSL's "conditions > pay" finding.

## Sources / citation
- **Chiu, E. Y. Y. & Fleming, T.**, *The Aesthetics of Disillusionment: Teachers' Narratives of "Disillusioned Transformation"*, **Behavioral Sciences, 2025**. Sample: 5 teachers (Hong Kong ×2, **Australia** ×1, France ×2), narrative/qualitative. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12292129/
- *Moral injury in teaching: the systemic roots of ethical conflict and emotional burnout in education*, **Educational Review, 2025** — qualitative study of **57 educators** (betrayal / ethical conflict / systemic pressure as central constructs). https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00131911.2025.2504523 ⚠️ full PDF access-gated this scan; the betrayal/attrition finding is summarised from the indexed abstract — re-verify via institutional access.
- Supporting: *An identity-informed narrative study of teacher attrition: why long-serving teachers leave* (Cambridge Journal of Education, 2025); *Why Teachers Leave: A Qualitative Exploration of Attrition and Retention* (2025).

## Faithful summary — key findings
- The exit is framed as **moral injury**, not simple burnout: the felt gap between the professional a teacher meant to be and the compliance role the system forces on them. Teachers are *"compelled to suppress authentic professional judgment"* — a recurring harm embedded in systems that devalue care and emotional expression.
- **Betrayal** is the pervasive theme in the n=57 study — disillusionment with leadership, unmet promises, systemic neglect — and it links significantly to depression, anxiety, burnout and **intention to leave**.
- **Invisibility / unheard voice**: participants describe leaders *"deaf to classroom realities"* — *"the ones leading the school and the ones in direct contact with students have very different interests"* (Amy). One participant (Beth) *"cried daily,"* unable to deliver the support she'd promised students under unrealistic demands.
- **Identity fracture**: teachers entered with passion for student development and found themselves serving institutional image instead; the wound is to *vocation and being-seen-as-a-professional*, not to the pay packet.
- The complementary US qualitative work names **diminished professional autonomy** alongside workload, support and pay as the four attrition drivers — autonomy being the identity-laden one.

## Why it matters (for any tenant; first used for ACME CO)
The non-obvious reframe: the teacher's deepest tension is **respect / professional dignity / a wounded calling**, sitting *beneath* the surveyed "workload" and well beneath "pay." A pay hook misreads it; even a "we'll lighten your load" hook is shallow. The register that lands is *being treated as a professional again*. For ACME CO this validates the supply-side promise (treat teachers as professionals; transparent pay; no umbrella skim) as on the deepest tension — proof-by-behaviour of respect. Universal because the "wounded vocation / moral injury" pattern recurs in any calling-driven workforce (nursing, social work, care).
