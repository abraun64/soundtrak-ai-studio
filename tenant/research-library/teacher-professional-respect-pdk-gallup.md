# Teacher Professional Respect — the esteem / respect gap (PDK & PDK-Gallup)

**Vertical**: universal · **Layer**: human-behaviour · **Audience**: educators · public · **Topic**: professional esteem / societal respect / values
**Added by**: Phase-1 insight scan — Run 2 (human layer), ACME CO Global Education Workforce Report, 2026-06-22.
**Segment relevance**: hard, current values anchor under the teacher (3.1) and relief-pool (3.2) human insights — makes the "respect gap" measurable.

## Sources / citation
- **PDK Poll of the Public's Attitudes Toward the Public Schools** (55th annual, 2023; produced by Langer Research Associates for PDK International). https://pdkpoll.org/2023-pdk-poll-results/
- **PDK / PDK-Gallup historical series** reported via EdWeek, *Teachers Are Stressed and Disrespected…* (2023). https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/teachers-are-stressed-and-disrespected-but-happier-than-last-year-7-takeaways-from-new-poll/2023/05
- Gallup, *Americans' Ratings of U.S. Professions* (2024) — grade-school teachers rank high on public trust (~61%) even as overall professional trust has fallen.

## Faithful summary — key data points
- Only **~55% of teachers say they feel respected by the general public as professionals** — **down from 77% in 2011** (PDK/Gallup series, via EdWeek 2023). Teachers feel more respected *within* their school communities and by parents than by the public at large.
- **75% of the public say teachers are undervalued in society** (PDK 2023); **66%** say local teacher salaries are too low; **58%** think teachers are overworked.
- Public agreement that teachers are *underpaid* is at its **highest level since 1969** (PDK long-run series) — more than double its 1981 low of 29%.
- The notable gap: the public *believes* teachers are undervalued, yet teachers' *felt* respect from that same public has fallen — the respect deficit is real and perceived on both sides.

## Why it matters (for any tenant; first used for ACME CO)
Turns the qualitative "wounded vocation / disrespect" finding into a hard, dated, quotable figure (55% vs 77%). For ACME CO it evidences that the candidate respect gap is not a vibe — it's measured and declining — so a campaign that *visibly respects teachers* speaks to a documented, felt deficit. Universal-adjacent: the cleanest example of a "professional esteem has eroded" values signal; the *method* (felt respect vs public perception) transfers to other professions facing status anxiety.
