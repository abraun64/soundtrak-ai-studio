## Faithful summary — key data points
- UK schools spend **~£1.4 billion a year on agency supply staff**.
- From **September 2026**, the ATH requires academy trusts to procure supply staff via the GCA RM6376 framework **unless they hold an alternative compliant arrangement at rates no higher than the framework**.
- **Supplier-fee caps** (the cap is on the *agency mark-up*, not on what workers are paid): STEM teachers £45/day · non-STEM £40/day · senior/leadership roles £55/day · educational support (non-SEND) £36 · SEND support £38 · admin/clerical £32 · other £34.
- Spend is **assessed/aggregated at trust level** (across all schools in the trust), not per-school — a structural shift that makes the *trust*, not the individual school, the buying and compliance unit.
- Worked savings examples cited: minimum daily saving of £26 per candidate; one school saved ~£150k over 18 months via a 12-week free temp-to-perm provision.
- Named sentiment (ASCL general secretary Pepe Di'Iasio, via Schools Week): "exorbitant fees being paid to supply agencies on a daily basis… a big drain on school finances." Costs to schools "are not always broken down transparently — with schools usually receiving a bulk daily charge or consolidated monthly invoice."

## Why it matters for ACME CO
A **dated, forced procurement reset** that flips long incumbent relationships from moat to liability and makes the *trust/network* the unit that must benchmark and justify spend. The report can be the instrument trusts use to answer "is our spend normal?" against a *national* figure — and ACME CO's transparent-pay / framework-listed position is on the right side of the reset. This is the single most time-sensitive hook in the scan (UK cut should lead the launch).
