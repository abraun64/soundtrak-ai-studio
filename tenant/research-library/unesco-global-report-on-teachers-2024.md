# UNESCO Global Report on Teachers (2024)

**Publisher**: UNESCO with the International Task Force on Teachers for Education 2030.
**Dates**: launched **February 2024**; re-surfaced at UNESCO World Summit on Teachers 2025.
**Added by**: Phase-1 insight scan (ACME CO Global Education Workforce Report), 2026-06-22.
**Segment relevance**: macro frame for all three segments (use as context, not as a headline insight — it's a global trend, see Insight Brief §2 kill register).

## Faithful summary — key data points
- The world needs **44 million additional teachers by 2030** to meet SDG education goals (primary 12.9M; secondary 31.1M — 7 of 10 needed teachers are secondary).
- **Europe & North America** need **4.8 million** additional teachers despite low birth rates (i.e. the shortage is not just a low-income-country story — it hits ACME CO's exact markets).
- **Primary-teacher annual attrition nearly doubled — from 4.62% (2015) to >9% (2022).**
- Estimated cost ~US$120bn/yr to cover new-teacher salaries by 2030.

## Why it matters / how to use it
This is the **macro backdrop**, not a campaign insight on its own — "global teacher shortage" is wallpaper every competitor can cite (see kill register #K1). Useful only as the *scale frame* a report opens on, or to size ACME CO's four-country footprint against a global number. The 4.8M Europe/NA figure is the most useful slice (it puts ACME CO's markets inside the crisis, countering "shortage is someone else's problem").
