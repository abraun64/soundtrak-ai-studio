## Faithful summary — key data points
- US districts could fill only **~54% of teacher absences** in late-2019/early-2020 (EdWeek Research Center); fill-rate trends are **down >10% since 2020** even as absences returned to pre-COVID levels.
- **35% of public schools were "extremely concerned"** about sourcing substitutes going into the 2024 school year; ~75% of school/district leaders said their need for subs was up while applications were down.
- Substitute pay median **~$17.97/hour**; dominant substitute sentiment is that economic pressure makes subbing **unsustainable**.
- Broader teacher economics (Gallup / NEA): teachers who say they are struggling are **2x as likely to hold a non-teaching side gig (46% vs 22%)**; average teacher salary 2024/25 ~$72,000 but public-school teachers earn **~27% less** than similarly-educated peers (the "teacher pay penalty").
